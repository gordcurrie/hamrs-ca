# B-001-008: Station Types and Qualification Requirements

## Where You Can Operate

Holders of an Amateur Radio Operator Certificate may operate **anywhere in Canada** — there is no geographic restriction tied to your home province or the address on your certificate.

## Qualification Requirements by Activity

Not all amateur radio activities require the same level of qualification. The regulations distinguish between activities that require only Basic, and those that require Basic **and** Advanced:

**Basic Qualification only:**
- Assemble commercially available transmitter kits of professional design
- Operate cross-band repeaters
- Operate remotely (changing frequency, mode, or power) — Note: this is a gotcha; remote operation actually requires Basic **and** Advanced
- Use single-band repeaters in voice mode — also requires Basic **and** Advanced (see below)

Let me be precise — the exam questions specify:

| Activity | Minimum Qualification |
|----------|----------------------|
| Assemble commercial transmitter kits | Basic |
| Install a single-band voice repeater | **Basic and Advanced** |
| Install an amateur radio club station | **Basic and Advanced** |
| Install/operate home-built transmitter or RF amplifier | **Basic and Advanced** |
| Operate cross-band repeaters | Basic |
| Remotely operate a transmitter (change freq/mode/power) | **Basic and Advanced** |

## Beacon Stations

**Beacon stations** are the one type of amateur station permitted to transmit one-way communications. All other amateur stations must engage in two-way communication.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Operators may operate **anywhere in Canada**
- Beacon stations may transmit **one-way communications**
- Installing a repeater, club station, or home-built transmitter/amplifier requires **Basic and Advanced**
- Assembling a commercial kit requires only **Basic**
- Cross-band repeater operation requires only **Basic**
- Remote operation (changing freq/mode/power) requires **Basic and Advanced**
