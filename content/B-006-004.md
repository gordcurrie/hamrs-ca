# B-006-004: Impedance Matching

## Why Match Impedance?

Maximum power is transferred from a source to a load when **source impedance equals load impedance** (the maximum power transfer theorem). In RF systems, mismatched impedances result in reflections, reduced efficiency, and transmitter stress.

The goal of **impedance matching** is to make the antenna system present 50 Ω to the transmitter.

## Antenna Tuner (ATU / Transmatch)

An **antenna tuner** is an adjustable LC network placed between the transmitter and feedline. By adjusting inductance and capacitance, it transforms the impedance the transmitter sees to 50 Ω, regardless of what the antenna actually presents.

Important: the ATU matches impedance at the shack end — it does not physically change the antenna impedance. High SWR on the feedline between tuner and antenna still causes feedline loss.

## Lambda/4 Matching Transformer

A **quarter-wave transformer** (λ/4 matching section) is a length of transmission line exactly one quarter-wavelength long that transforms impedance:

> **Z_match = √(Z_source × Z_load)**

Example: to match a 200 Ω antenna to 50 Ω coax:  
Z_match = √(200 × 50) = √10000 = **100 Ω** — use 100 Ω coax for the quarter-wave section.

## Balun

A **balun** (balanced-to-unbalanced transformer) is used when connecting a balanced antenna (like a dipole) to an unbalanced feedline (like coaxial cable). Without a balun, RF current can flow on the outside of the coax braid, causing:
- Pattern distortion
- RF in the shack
- Interference to other equipment

A **1:1 balun** matches equal impedances (just converts balanced/unbalanced)  
A **4:1 balun** transforms 200 Ω balanced to 50 Ω unbalanced (common for folded dipoles)

## L-Network, Pi-Network, T-Network

Standard impedance matching networks used in amplifier output circuits and antenna tuners:
- **L-network**: two components (one L, one C) — simplest
- **Pi-network**: three components — used in tube amplifier output stages; adjustable Q
- **T-network**: three components — common in solid-state tuners

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Impedance matching purpose: **maximum power transfer** (minimize reflections)
- Antenna tuner: adjustable LC network that matches **transmitter to feedline**
- Antenna tuner does NOT: **reduce feedline loss** caused by high SWR
- Quarter-wave transformer: Z_match = **√(Z_source × Z_load)**
- Balun purpose: connect **balanced antenna to unbalanced feedline**
- Without balun on dipole: RF current on **outside of coax braid**
- 4:1 balun: transforms **200 Ω to 50 Ω**
