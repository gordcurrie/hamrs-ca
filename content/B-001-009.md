# B-001-009: Control Operators and Station Responsibility

## The Control Operator

Every amateur radio station must have a **control operator** whenever it is transmitting. The control operator must be **at the station's control point** — not just nearby, but at the point from which the station is being controlled.

The control operator must be a **qualified amateur radio operator chosen by the station owner**. You cannot designate someone without a certificate as control operator.

## Shared Responsibility

Responsibility in the amateur service is dual. When you transmit from another person's station:
- **Both you (the control operator) and the station owner** are responsible for the operation of the station

This cannot be avoided. If you make a transmission that violates the regulations from someone else's station, you are both on the hook.

## Unqualified Persons

An unqualified person (no certificate) may operate an amateur station only under one condition: they must be **under the supervision and in the presence of** a certificate holder. The certificate holder must be physically present — remote supervision by phone doesn't count for this purpose. The certificate holder remains the control operator and bears responsibility.

Family members without certificates cannot transmit using your station when you are not physically present with them, even if you own the station. They are not qualified to be control operators.

## Station Owner Responsibility

Even if you are not the control operator, as the station owner you are responsible for ensuring that operation conforms to the regulations. You cannot escape responsibility by delegating control to someone else — both parties share it.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Station must have a control operator **whenever transmitting**
- Control operator must be **at the control point** when transmitting
- Control operator: any **qualified** amateur chosen by the station owner
- Transmitting from another's station: **both** the control operator and station owner are responsible
- Unqualified person may operate: only **under supervision and in the physical presence** of a certificate holder
