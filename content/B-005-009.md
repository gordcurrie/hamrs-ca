# B-005-009: Capacitors

## What is a Capacitor?

A **capacitor** stores energy in an **electric field** between two conductive plates separated by an insulator (the **dielectric**). The property is **capacitance (C)**, measured in **farads (F)**. Practical values are in the picofarad (pF) to microfarad (μF) range.

## How a Capacitor Behaves

A capacitor **opposes changes in voltage**:
- With steady DC: after charging, a capacitor looks like an **open circuit** (no DC current flows once fully charged)
- With AC: the capacitor presents **capacitive reactance** (passes AC, opposing voltage changes)

This is the opposite of an inductor: capacitors block DC and pass AC; inductors pass DC and block high-frequency AC.

## Capacitive Reactance

The opposition a capacitor presents to AC is:

> **X_C = 1 / (2π × f × C)**

Where:
- X_C = capacitive reactance in ohms (Ω)
- f = frequency in Hz
- C = capacitance in farads

As frequency **increases**, X_C **decreases** — capacitors pass high frequencies more easily.  
As C increases, X_C decreases.

## Combining Capacitors

- **Parallel capacitors**: C_total = C1 + C2 + … (values add — same as series resistors)
- **Series capacitors**: 1/C_total = 1/C1 + 1/C2 + … (same as parallel resistors — total is less than smallest)

## Types of Capacitors

- **Ceramic**: small values, good for RF (low loss, stable)
- **Electrolytic**: large values (μF to mF), polarized, used for power supply filtering; must be connected with correct polarity
- **Mica / Silver mica**: stable, accurate — used in RF tuned circuits
- **Variable capacitor**: tuning capacitor for receiver/transmitter VFOs

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Capacitor stores energy in: **electric field**
- Capacitance unit: **farads (F)**
- Capacitive reactance: **X_C = 1 / (2πfC)**
- As frequency increases, X_C: **decreases**
- Capacitor at DC: acts as **open circuit**
- Parallel capacitors: capacitance **adds**
- Series capacitors: total capacitance **decreases**
- Electrolytic capacitors are: **polarized** (must observe polarity)
