# B-006-001: Transmission Line Fundamentals

## What is a Transmission Line?

A **transmission line** is a structure designed to carry RF energy from a transmitter to an antenna (or from an antenna to a receiver) with minimum loss. Common types are **coaxial cable** and **open-wire (ladder) line**.

## Characteristic Impedance

Every transmission line has a **characteristic impedance (Z₀)** determined by its physical dimensions and dielectric material — not by its length. This impedance is an intrinsic property of the cable type.

Common impedances:
- **50 Ω** — standard coaxial cable for amateur radio transmitters/receivers
- **75 Ω** — standard coaxial cable for TV/cable systems
- **300 Ω** — twin-lead (flat ribbon) for TV antennas
- **450–600 Ω** — open-wire ladder line

## Wave Propagation on a Transmission Line

RF energy travels along the transmission line as a **wave**. The **velocity factor (VF)** is the speed of the wave in the cable expressed as a fraction of the speed of light. A cable with VF = 0.66 propagates signals at 66% of the speed of light in vacuum.

This matters for calculating **electrical length** of feedlines and phasing stubs.

## Matched Line

When the load impedance equals the line's characteristic impedance (Z_load = Z₀), all power is absorbed by the load — **no reflections** occur. This is the ideal matched condition: SWR = 1:1.

## Mismatched Line and Reflections

When Z_load ≠ Z₀, some power is **reflected** back toward the source. The reflected wave and forward wave create **standing waves** on the line. The ratio of reflected to forward voltage is characterized by **SWR** (see B-006-003).

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Transmission line purpose: carry RF energy **with minimum loss**
- Characteristic impedance: determined by **physical dimensions** (not length)
- Amateur radio coax standard: **50 Ω**
- TV coax standard: **75 Ω**
- Ladder line: **300–600 Ω**
- Matched condition: Z_load = Z₀ → **no reflections**, SWR = 1:1
- Velocity factor: speed of wave in cable as **fraction of speed of light**
