# B-006-011: Antenna Feed Systems

## Direct Feed

The simplest method: coaxial cable connected directly to the centre of a dipole. Works well when antenna feedpoint impedance ≈ 50 Ω. For a dipole (≈72 Ω), the mismatch produces SWR ≈ 1.4:1 — acceptable for most purposes.

## Coaxial Feed with Balun

A **dipole** is a **balanced** antenna (equal and opposite currents in each half). Coaxial cable is **unbalanced** (current is unequal on inner and outer conductors). Without a **balun**, RF current flows on the outside of the coax braid, causing:
- Radiation from the feedline (distorts pattern)
- RF in the shack
- Potential interference

A **1:1 balun** at the feedpoint fixes this by making the connection balanced.

## Open-Wire Feed (Ladder Line)

**Ladder line** (open-wire line, window line, twin-lead) has very low loss even at high SWR — loss is primarily in the dielectric, which is mostly air. This makes it ideal for feeding a dipole on multiple bands with an antenna tuner.

The feedline is run to the shack where a **balanced antenna tuner** (or a balun + unbalanced tuner) matches the impedance on each band.

## End Feed

**End-fed antennas** are fed at one end rather than the centre. The high impedance (2000–5000 Ω) requires a matching transformer (typically 49:1 or 64:1 unun) to match 50 Ω coax. Popular for portable/SOTA operation because no centre support is needed.

## Delta Feed and Gamma Match

Used to match the low impedance of a Yagi driven element to 50 Ω coax without a balun:
- **Gamma match**: adjustable tap on the driven element with a series capacitor
- **Delta match**: two separate connection points on the driven element

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Dipole is: **balanced** (equal opposite currents in each half)
- Coax is: **unbalanced**
- Without balun on dipole: RF flows on **outside of coax braid**
- 1:1 balun function: converts **balanced to unbalanced**
- Open-wire feedline advantage: **very low loss even at high SWR**
- End-fed antenna: high feedpoint impedance requires **matching transformer**
- Ladder line: must be kept away from **metal** (metal degrades the balance)
