# B-006-013: Antenna Bandwidth and Special Antenna Types

## Antenna Bandwidth

**Bandwidth** is the range of frequencies over which an antenna's SWR remains below an acceptable limit (typically 2:1). Outside this range, SWR rises and the antenna becomes less efficient without retuning.

Factors affecting bandwidth:
- **Thicker elements**: wider bandwidth (lower Q)
- **Higher Q / resonant circuits**: narrower bandwidth
- **Multi-band traps**: allow operation on multiple bands with a single antenna

## Trap Antenna

A **trap antenna** uses **parallel LC circuits** (traps) inserted at specific points along the antenna wire. Each trap acts as a **parallel resonant circuit** that presents very high impedance at its resonant frequency, electrically shortening the antenna on that band. Below the trap's frequency, the trap is transparent (low impedance) and the full length operates on lower bands.

Common configuration: 40/20/10 m trap dipole — two sets of traps allow operation on three bands without an antenna tuner.

## The G5RV Antenna

The **G5RV** is a multiband dipole approximately 31 metres long fed with a specific length of open-wire line, then coax. With an antenna tuner, it covers most HF bands from 3.5–30 MHz. It is popular because it is simple and inexpensive.

## The Quad and Loop Antenna

A **full-wave loop** (quad or delta loop) resonates at one wavelength. It has approximately **2 dBd gain** over a dipole when oriented correctly, and lower feedpoint impedance makes it naturally more broadband than a dipole.

**Quad antenna**: a square loop — two half-wave horizontal sections (top and bottom) and two vertical sections. Full-wave on the operating frequency. Can be stacked (cubical quad) for additional gain like a Yagi.

## The Discone Antenna

A **discone** is a wideband vertical antenna used for VHF/UHF reception. It has very wide frequency coverage (10:1 or more) making it popular for scanner antennas. Gain is modest (0 dBd) but the bandwidth is exceptional.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Trap antenna: uses **parallel LC circuits** to achieve multiband operation
- Trap function at resonant frequency: presents **high impedance** (electrically shortens antenna)
- Thicker antenna elements: **wider bandwidth**
- Full-wave loop gain: approximately **2 dBd** over a dipole
- G5RV: **multiband dipole** fed with open-wire section and coax
- Discone: **wideband** VHF/UHF antenna (popular for scanning)
- Bandwidth vs. Q: lower Q = **wider bandwidth**
