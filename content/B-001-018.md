# B-001-018: Repeaters and Special Transmission Types

## Repeaters

A **repeater station** automatically retransmits the signals of other stations. Repeaters extend the range of portable and mobile stations by receiving on one frequency and simultaneously retransmitting on another (the "offset").

**Who can use repeaters**: Holders of Basic Qualification (without HF access) may use repeaters, but only repeaters whose **output frequency** is 29.5 MHz or higher. The lowest output frequency segment accessible with Basic only is **29.500–29.700 MHz** (the 10-metre FM segment). All VHF and UHF repeaters are within reach.

This is an important nuance: Basic holders cannot use HF frequencies directly, but they can use a 10-metre FM repeater as long as they're listening on its output (above 29.5 MHz).

## Unmodulated Carriers

An unmodulated carrier — a continuous wave with no information content — may be transmitted only:
- **For brief tests** (not extended testing)
- **On frequencies below 30 MHz**

Transmitting a dead carrier on VHF or UHF without modulation for anything other than an extremely brief test is not permitted.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Repeater station: **automatically retransmits** signals of other stations
- Basic Qualification holders may use repeaters with output **at or above 29.500 MHz**
- Lowest accessible repeater segment for Basic only: **29.500–29.700 MHz**
- Unmodulated carrier: permitted only for **brief tests** and only on **frequencies below 30 MHz**
