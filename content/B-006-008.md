# B-006-008: Yagi Antennas

## What is a Yagi?

A **Yagi-Uda antenna** (commonly called a Yagi) is a **directional** antenna consisting of multiple parallel elements:

1. **Driven element**: the only element connected to the feedline — usually a half-wave dipole
2. **Reflector**: one element slightly longer than the driven element, placed **behind** it (away from the desired direction)
3. **Directors**: one or more elements slightly shorter than the driven element, placed **in front** of it (toward the desired direction)

The parasitic elements (reflector and directors) are not connected to the feedline — they interact with the driven element through electromagnetic coupling, shaping the radiation pattern.

## How a Yagi Works

The reflector and directors act as passive resonators that re-radiate energy in a controlled phase relationship with the driven element. The result is **constructive interference** in the forward direction and **destructive interference** in the rearward direction.

## Gain

A Yagi provides **forward gain** over a reference antenna (usually an isotropic radiator or a dipole):
- A **3-element Yagi**: typically 6–8 dBd gain (dB over a dipole)
- More directors = more gain = narrower beamwidth
- Longer boom = higher gain (elements spread further apart)

## Front-to-Back Ratio

The **front-to-back ratio (F/B)** measures how much more strongly a Yagi radiates in the forward direction vs. the rearward direction. A high F/B ratio means good rejection of signals from the rear — important for directional listening.

## Applications

- **HF DX antennas**: 3- or 4-element Yagis on rotatable towers
- **VHF/UHF work**: compact multi-element Yagis (15+ elements for EME)
- **Satellite tracking**: high-gain Yagi or crossed-Yagi arrays
- **Direction finding**: Yagis identify signal direction by rotating for maximum or null

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Yagi driven element: **connected to feedline** (half-wave dipole)
- Reflector: slightly **longer** than driven element, placed **behind**
- Directors: slightly **shorter** than driven element, placed **in front**
- Gain vs. dipole: increases with **more directors and longer boom**
- Radiation: **directional** (maximum forward, minimum rearward)
- Front-to-back ratio: measures **rejection of signals from the rear**
- 3-element Yagi gain: approximately **6–8 dBd**
