# B-006-005: Antenna Polarization

## What is Polarization?

**Polarization** refers to the orientation of the electric field of the radiated electromagnetic wave. It is determined by the orientation of the antenna elements:

- **Horizontal polarization**: antenna elements are horizontal → electric field is horizontal
- **Vertical polarization**: antenna elements are vertical → electric field is vertical
- **Circular polarization**: the electric field rotates as the wave propagates (used for satellite work)

## Why Polarization Matters

For best signal transfer between two stations, their antennas should have **matching polarization**. A vertical transmitting antenna and a horizontal receiving antenna produce a **cross-polarization loss** of approximately 20 dB — the received signal is dramatically weaker.

## VHF/UHF Conventions

- **FM repeaters and simplex (VHF/UHF)**: typically **vertical polarization**. Handheld antennas (rubber duck, whip) are vertical, so vertically polarized repeaters match naturally.
- **SSB and weak-signal VHF**: typically **horizontal polarization**. Yagi arrays for EME (moonbounce) and weak-signal work are typically mounted with horizontal elements.
- **Satellite communication**: uses **circular polarization** (or the station must be able to rotate polarization) because the satellite's polarization relative to the ground changes with orbital position.

## HF Propagation and Polarization

On HF, the ionosphere scrambles polarization during sky-wave propagation — by the time the signal arrives via skip, the polarization is essentially random. Polarization matching matters much less for HF skip contacts, though it still matters for HF ground-wave contacts.

## Antenna Orientation

The polarization of a simple wire antenna:
- **Horizontal dipole**: horizontal polarization
- **Vertical whip**: vertical polarization
- **Inverted-V dipole**: primarily horizontal polarization (elements slope but are mostly horizontal)

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Polarization determined by: **orientation of antenna elements**
- Vertical antenna → **vertical polarization**
- Horizontal antenna → **horizontal polarization**
- Cross-polarization loss: approximately **20 dB**
- VHF/UHF FM: conventional polarization is **vertical**
- VHF/UHF SSB/weak-signal: conventional polarization is **horizontal**
- Satellite work: uses **circular polarization**
- HF sky-wave: polarization is **scrambled by ionosphere** (matching less critical)
