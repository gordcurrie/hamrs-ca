# B-003-002: FM Transmitter Stages

## How FM Modulation Works

In an FM transmitter, audio information is encoded by varying the **frequency** of the carrier — rather than varying its amplitude (AM) or phase. When you speak into the microphone, the louder and higher the audio, the greater the frequency deviation of the carrier.

## Stage-by-Stage

**Microphone**: Converts air pressure changes (sound) into an electrical signal. Produces a low-level audio signal that drives the speech amplifier.

**Speech amplifier**: Amplifies the microphone audio to the level needed by the modulator.

**Modulator**: Varies the frequency of the oscillator based on the audio input. This is what makes FM fundamentally different from AM/CW/SSB — the oscillator frequency itself is being shifted by the audio signal.

**Oscillator**: Generates the carrier. In FM, the modulator directly affects the oscillator frequency. In AM, CW, and SSB transmitters, the oscillator runs at a fixed frequency — the FM oscillator is uniquely modulated.

**Frequency multiplier**: Takes the oscillator output and multiplies its frequency to reach the final transmit frequency. In doing so, it also multiplies the frequency deviation — so a small deviation at the oscillator becomes the correct deviation at the output frequency. The frequency multiplier produces a **useful harmonic** (intentionally generating and using a multiple of the input frequency).

**Power amplifier (PA)**: Increases output power to the desired transmit level. This stage draws the **most electric power** of any stage in the transmitter.

## Key Distinction: FM Oscillator vs. Other Modes

In AM, CW, and SSB transmitters, the oscillator runs at a fixed frequency and the signal is modified elsewhere (balanced modulator, keying circuit, etc.). In an FM transmitter, the **modulator alters the oscillator frequency directly**. This is the fundamental architectural difference.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Microphone: produces electrical signal from **air pressure changes**
- Modulator: **affects the frequency of the oscillator**
- FM oscillator differs from AM/CW/SSB: the **modulator alters its frequency**
- Frequency multiplier: **produces the FM output carrier frequency** by using a useful harmonic
- Power amplifier: **draws the most electric power** of any stage
