# B-006-012: Mobile and Portable Antennas

## Mobile Antenna Challenges

A vehicle places unique constraints on antenna design:
- **Limited height**: a full quarter-wave at HF is impractically long (e.g., 40 m band → 10 m whip)
- **Vehicle body as ground plane**: the car roof, hood, or trunk provides the ground plane radials
- **Mechanical stress**: vibration and wind require robust mounting

## Mobile HF Antennas

**Shortened whip with loading coil**: A physically short antenna is electrically lengthened by adding a **loading coil** — an inductor that adds inductive reactance to compensate for the capacitive reactance of a short antenna. Coil placement:
- **Base loading**: coil at the base — simplest, lowest efficiency
- **Centre loading**: coil mid-whip — higher efficiency
- **Top loading**: coil at the top — best efficiency (but mechanically awkward)

A **top hat** (horizontal wire or disc at the tip) also adds capacitance to electrically lengthen the antenna and is often combined with loading coils.

## Mobile VHF/UHF Antennas

**5/8 wave vertical**: common for 2 m and 70 cm mobile; slightly higher gain than a quarter-wave and has a naturally low radiation angle. Typically a direct coax feed with a matching network.

**Magnetic mount (mag-mount)**: uses a strong magnet to attach to the vehicle roof; the metal roof forms the ground plane. Easy installation, but the coax runs through a door or window.

## Portable Antennas

For field and portable operation:
- **Telescoping whips**: adjustable to resonance at different bands
- **Wire dipoles**: light and compact, strung between trees
- **End-fed half-wave (EFHW)** with 49:1 transformer: popular for SOTA/POTA — one feedpoint support, one end tied to a tree
- **Magnetic loop**: compact, low-noise, but narrow bandwidth (requires frequent retuning)

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Short mobile HF antenna: electrically lengthened with **loading coil**
- Loading coil position for best efficiency: **top loading**
- Vehicle body function: serves as **ground plane**
- 5/8 wave vertical: provides **slightly higher gain** than quarter-wave
- Mag-mount: uses vehicle roof as **ground plane**
- EFHW matching: requires **49:1 transformer** to match 50 Ω coax
- Magnetic loop antenna: **narrow bandwidth**, requires frequent retuning
