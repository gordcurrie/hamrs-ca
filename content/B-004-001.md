# B-004-001: Amplifiers

## What an Amplifier Does

An amplifier is a circuit designed to **increase the amplitude of a signal**. Specifically, it can increase voltage, current, or power — or all three. The increase in signal level is called **gain**. A device labelled "Gain = 10 dB" is an amplifier.

A device with gain has the property of **amplification**.

## Amplifier Applications

- **RF amplifier**: Increases the level of very weak radio signals from an antenna before further processing
- **Audio amplifier**: Increases the level of very weak signals from a microphone
- **Speech amplifier**: Processes the 300–3000 Hz voice range in a transceiver

## Linearity

A **linear** amplifier produces an output that is a faithful scaled copy of the input — the relationship between input and output is a straight line (proportional). If an amplifier becomes **non-linear**, the output signal becomes **distorted** — new frequency components are generated that were not in the original signal. For SSB voice this is catastrophic; the voice becomes unintelligible. For general signals, distortion creates spurious emissions.

## Efficiency

**Efficiency** is the ratio of **output power to DC input power** of an amplifier. A perfectly efficient amplifier would convert all DC input to RF output. In reality, the difference is dissipated as heat. High-efficiency amplifiers (Class D, Class E) run cool; lower-efficiency (Class A) run hot.

## Positive Feedback and Oscillation

**Positive feedback** — feeding a portion of the output back to the input in phase — causes **oscillations** in an amplifier stage. The gain of the feedback loop exceeds 1, the signal grows, and the amplifier oscillates at the feedback frequency. This is how oscillators are built intentionally, but in an amplifier it is a fault. Excessive positive feedback causes the amplifier to stop amplifying and start oscillating instead.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Amplifier: designed to **increase the amplitude of a signal**
- Gain: the **increase in signal level** by an amplifier
- "Gain = 10 dB" identifies: an **amplifier**
- Non-linear amplifier output: **distorted**
- RF amplifier: increases level of **weak radio signals from antenna**
- Audio amplifier: increases level of **weak signals from microphone**
- Speech amplifier processes: **300 Hz to 3000 Hz**
- Efficiency: ratio of **output power to DC input power**
- Excessive positive feedback: causes **oscillations**
