# B-005-011: Transformers

## What a Transformer Does

A **transformer** transfers AC electrical energy between two circuits through **mutual induction** (magnetic coupling) while changing voltage and current levels. It works only with AC — a steady DC produces a constant magnetic field that induces no voltage in the secondary.

## Construction

A transformer has:
- **Primary winding**: connected to the AC source — input
- **Secondary winding**: connected to the load — output
- **Core**: usually laminated iron (power frequencies) or ferrite (RF) — concentrates the magnetic flux

## The Turns Ratio

The ratio of turns determines how voltage is transformed:

> **V_primary / V_secondary = N_primary / N_secondary**

Or: **V_s = V_p × (N_s / N_p)**

- **Step-up transformer**: N_s > N_p → voltage increases
- **Step-down transformer**: N_s < N_p → voltage decreases

**Example**: Primary 120 V, 10 turns primary, 5 turns secondary → secondary voltage = 120 × (5/10) = **60 V** (step-down)

## Current Transformation

Energy is conserved (ideal transformer): if voltage steps up, current steps down proportionally.

> **I_primary / I_secondary = N_secondary / N_primary**

(Turns ratio is inverted for current vs. voltage)

## Impedance Transformation

A transformer also transforms impedance:

> **Z_primary / Z_secondary = (N_primary / N_secondary)²**

This is the key use in RF: matching the output impedance of a transmitter (50 Ω) to an antenna or other load.

## RF Transformers

In RF circuits, transformers are used for:
- **Impedance matching** between stages
- **Baluns** (balanced-to-unbalanced conversion) at antenna feedpoints
- **Galvanic isolation** between circuits

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Transformer works on: **AC only** (DC produces no changing flux)
- Turns ratio determines: **voltage transformation**
- Step-up: more secondary turns → **voltage increases, current decreases**
- Step-down: fewer secondary turns → **voltage decreases, current increases**
- Impedance ratio: **(N_p/N_s)²** (turns ratio squared)
- Primary 240 V, 4:1 step-down → secondary = **60 V**
- Balun function: converts **balanced to unbalanced** (or vice versa)
