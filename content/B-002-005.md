# B-002-005: CW (Morse Code) Procedures

## CQ on Morse Code

The CQ procedure in CW parallels voice but with specific Morse conventions:
- Send **"CQ"** three times
- Send **"DE"** (meaning "from" in Morse operating practice)
- Send your **call sign** three times

Example: `CQ CQ CQ DE VA3RAC VA3RAC VA3RAC K`

## Answering a CQ on CW

When answering a routine CW CQ:
- Send the other station's **call sign twice**
- Send **"DE"**
- Send **your call sign twice**

Note the difference from voice: on CW, the calling station's sign is repeated twice in the answer (not once like in voice).

## Speed

Transmit a CQ at **any speed you can reliably receive**. If you call at 20 wpm and can't copy the response, you've created a problem. Calling speed signals the responder what speed to use.

## Key Morse Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **CQ** | Calling any station |
| **DE** | From (this is...) |
| **K** | Any station please reply (go ahead) |
| **R** | All received (roger) |
| **QSK** | Full break-in CW — received signals can be heard between dots and dashes |
| **DX** | Distant station (long-distance contact) |
| **73** | Best regards |

## Frequency Separation for CW

When selecting a CW transmitting frequency near a contact already in progress, allow **150–500 Hz** separation. CW signals are extremely narrow — a few hundred Hz is enough to keep them distinct when using narrow filters.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- CW CQ format: **"CQ" three times + "DE" + call sign three times**
- Answering CW CQ: **other call sign twice + "DE" + your call sign twice**
- CQ speed: **any speed you can reliably receive**
- DE: **from**
- K: **any station please reply**
- R: **all received**
- DX: **distant station**
- 73: **best regards**
- QSK: **full break-in CW** — signals heard between elements
- CW frequency separation: **150–500 Hz**
