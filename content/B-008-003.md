# B-008-003: Audio Rectification and Consumer Equipment Interference

## What is Audio Rectification?

**Audio rectification** (also called RF rectification) occurs when RF energy enters audio equipment — a stereo amplifier, telephone, intercom, or speaker — and the RF signal is demodulated (detected) by non-linear junctions inside the equipment. The result is **audio from the RF signal being heard** on the audio device.

If you transmit voice (SSB or FM) and someone's stereo plays your audio back at them, that is audio rectification.

## Why This Happens

Modern consumer electronics are not designed to be immune to strong RF fields. Internal semiconductor junctions (diodes, transistor base-emitter junctions, IC input protection diodes) act as RF detectors when RF enters on the audio wiring, speaker leads, or power supply connections.

## Who is at Fault?

**Audio rectification is usually the fault of the affected equipment**, not the amateur station, provided the amateur is:
- Operating within licence privileges
- Not radiating excessive harmonics or spurious emissions
- Keeping fundamental emissions reasonable for the location

However, in practice, the amateur is expected to **cooperate** in resolving the problem even if not technically responsible.

## Solutions for Audio Rectification

**At the affected equipment** (the preferred fix):
- **Ferrite choke**: clip-on ferrite core on the affected audio cables, speaker leads, or power cord — attenuates RF without affecting audio
- **Bypass capacitors**: small ceramic capacitors across input lines to shunt RF to ground

**At the amateur station** (supplementary):
- Reduce power if possible
- Improve antenna directivity away from the affected equipment
- Use a better ground on the station equipment
- Ensure feedline is not radiating (use balun, check coax shield)

## Telephone Interference

Telephones are particularly susceptible. RF can enter via:
- The telephone line itself (acts as antenna)
- The handset cord

Ferrite cores on the telephone line at the entry point are the most effective solution.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Audio rectification: RF is **demodulated by non-linear junctions** inside audio equipment
- Fault: usually the **consumer equipment's** insufficient RF immunity (not the amateur)
- Amateur's transmit must be: within privileges, no excessive spurious emissions
- Best fix for audio rectification: **ferrite choke on affected cables**
- Ferrite choke: attenuates **RF without affecting audio quality**
- Telephone interference: ferrite on **telephone line at point of entry**
- Bypass capacitor: **shunts RF to ground** at equipment input
