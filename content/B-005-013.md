# B-005-013: Electrical Measurement

## The Multimeter

A **multimeter** (VOM — volt-ohm-milliammeter) measures voltage, current, and resistance. It is the essential test instrument for every station.

**Voltmeter**: measures voltage — connected **in parallel** with the component being measured. An ideal voltmeter has **infinite resistance** (draws no current from the circuit).

**Ammeter**: measures current — connected **in series** with the circuit. An ideal ammeter has **zero resistance** (does not drop voltage). **Never connect an ammeter in parallel** across a voltage source — it has near-zero resistance and will pass enormous current.

**Ohmmeter**: measures resistance — used only on **de-energized** circuits. Applying an ohmmeter to a live circuit will give incorrect readings and may damage it.

## SWR Meter

An **SWR meter** (or VSWR meter) measures **standing wave ratio** — the ratio of forward to reflected power in a transmission line. Used at the feedline to check antenna match. Connected **in series** with the feedline between the transmitter and antenna.

## Wattmeter

A **wattmeter** measures RF output power. Like the SWR meter, it connects **in series** with the feedline. Directional wattmeters measure forward and reflected power separately.

## Oscilloscope

An **oscilloscope** displays voltage vs. time — it shows the waveform. Used to:
- Observe signal shape (check for distortion)
- Measure frequency (from the period)
- Measure peak voltage
- Check for spurious oscillations

The oscilloscope is connected **in parallel** (high impedance probe).

## Frequency Counter

A **frequency counter** measures the exact frequency of a signal. Essential for checking VFO calibration and verifying transmit frequency.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Voltmeter connection: **in parallel** with the component
- Ideal voltmeter resistance: **infinite** (draws no current)
- Ammeter connection: **in series** with the circuit
- Ideal ammeter resistance: **zero** (no voltage drop)
- Never connect ammeter: **in parallel across a source** (short circuit hazard)
- Ohmmeter: use only on **de-energized** circuits
- SWR meter position: **in series** between transmitter and antenna
- Oscilloscope: displays **voltage vs. time** (waveform)
- Frequency counter: measures **exact frequency**
