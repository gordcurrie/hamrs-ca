# B-006-010: The Radio Wave and Electromagnetic Radiation

## What is an Electromagnetic Wave?

An **electromagnetic (EM) wave** consists of oscillating electric and magnetic fields propagating through space at the **speed of light** (c ≈ 300,000,000 m/s or 3 × 10⁸ m/s). These fields are perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation.

Radio waves are electromagnetic waves in the **3 Hz to 300 GHz** range — the radio frequency (RF) spectrum.

## The Speed of Light

In **free space (vacuum)**:  
> **c = 300,000 km/s = 300,000,000 m/s ≈ 3 × 10⁸ m/s**

Radio waves travel at this speed through air (very close to c). In materials, the speed is reduced by the **velocity factor**.

## Frequency–Wavelength Relationship

> **λ = c / f**   or in practical units:   **λ (m) = 300 / f (MHz)**

This is the most important relationship in antenna work:

| Band    | Frequency | Wavelength |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| 80 m    | 3.5 MHz   | ≈ 86 m    |
| 40 m    | 7 MHz     | ≈ 43 m    |
| 20 m    | 14 MHz    | ≈ 21 m    |
| 10 m    | 28 MHz    | ≈ 10.7 m  |
| 2 m     | 144 MHz   | ≈ 2.08 m  |
| 70 cm   | 440 MHz   | ≈ 68 cm   |

## Near Field and Far Field

Close to an antenna (within a few wavelengths), the **near field** is complex — electric and magnetic fields are not yet fully coupled into a propagating wave. Beyond several wavelengths, the **far field** behaves like a true EM wave and follows the inverse square law.

## Inverse Square Law

Power density in the far field decreases as the **square of the distance**:

> **P_d ∝ 1 / d²**

Double the distance → power density drops to **one quarter**.

This is important for both link budget calculations and RF safety assessments.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Speed of radio waves in free space: **300,000 km/s (3 × 10⁸ m/s)**
- Wavelength formula: **λ (m) = 300 / f (MHz)**
- 28 MHz wavelength: 300/28 ≈ **10.7 m**
- 144 MHz wavelength: 300/144 ≈ **2.08 m**
- Power density decreases with distance: **inverse square law** (1/d²)
- Double distance → power density = **one quarter**
- EM wave components: **electric field, magnetic field** — perpendicular to each other and to propagation direction
