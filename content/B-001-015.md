# B-001-015: Operating Privileges and HF Band Access

## Operating Privileges Are Tied to Your Qualifications

A critical rule: **your operating privileges are limited by your own qualifications**, regardless of the station you are using or who is the control operator.

If you allow someone with higher qualifications to use your station, they **cannot** exercise their higher privileges under your call sign — they are limited to your qualifications. Conversely, if you operate at someone else's station and they hold higher qualifications, you are still limited to what your qualifications authorize. The lower qualification is the ceiling.

## HF Access — The 80% / Honours Rule

By default, Basic Qualification holders do not have access to frequencies below 30 MHz (the HF bands: 160m, 80m, 40m, 20m, 15m, 10m, etc.). To gain HF access, you must satisfy one of these conditions:

- Score **80% or higher** on the Basic examination (this is called Honours), OR
- Hold an **Advanced Qualification**, OR
- Hold a **Morse code Qualification**

Any one of these three unlocks HF. The Basic exam score matters — 80% and above gives you HF; below 80% does not.

## Canadian HF Band Limits (memorize these)

| Band | Frequency Range |
|------|----------------|
| 160 metres | 1.8 MHz to 2.0 MHz |
| 80 metres | 3.5 MHz to 4.0 MHz |
| 40 metres | 7.0 MHz to 7.3 MHz |
| 20 metres | 14.000 MHz to 14.350 MHz |
| 15 metres | 21.000 MHz to 21.450 MHz |
| 10 metres | 28.000 MHz to 29.700 MHz |

## Model Radio Control

Basic Qualification holders may operate radio-controlled models on **all amateur radio bands above 30 MHz**. There is no HF access for model control; it is VHF/UHF only for Basic holders who haven't qualified for HF.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Your privileges = **your qualifications**, regardless of station or other operator present
- HF access requires: **80% on Basic** (Honours), or **Advanced**, or **Morse code** qualification
- 160m: **1.8–2.0 MHz**; 80m: **3.5–4.0 MHz**; 40m: **7.0–7.3 MHz**
- 20m: **14.000–14.350 MHz**; 15m: **21.000–21.450 MHz**; 10m: **28.000–29.700 MHz**
- Model control: Basic holders may use **all bands above 30 MHz**
