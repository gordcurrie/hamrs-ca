# B-006-009: Antenna Gain and the Beam Antenna

## What is Antenna Gain?

**Antenna gain** is a measure of how effectively an antenna focuses radiated power in a particular direction compared to a reference antenna. Gain does not mean the antenna creates power — it **redistributes** it. Power is concentrated in the desired direction at the expense of other directions.

## Reference Antennas

Gain is always stated relative to a reference:

- **dBi** — gain relative to an **isotropic radiator** (theoretical perfect sphere)
- **dBd** — gain relative to a **half-wave dipole** (a real, practical reference)

Relationship: **dBi = dBd + 2.15** (a dipole has 2.15 dBi gain over an isotropic radiator)

## Effective Radiated Power (ERP)

**ERP (Effective Radiated Power)** accounts for both transmitter power and antenna gain:

> **ERP = Transmitter Power × Antenna Gain** (as a power ratio)

Or in decibels: **ERP(dBW) = P_tx(dBW) + Gain(dBd)**

Example: 100 W transmitter + 3-element Yagi (7 dBd gain) → ERP = 100 × 5 = **500 W ERP**

ISED regulations specify maximum ERP for many licence classes and frequency bands.

## Beamwidth

A high-gain antenna has a **narrower beamwidth** — the angle within which the antenna radiates effectively. This is the tradeoff: gain comes at the cost of coverage angle. A Yagi must be pointed at the target station.

**Half-power beamwidth (HPBW)**: the angle between the points where power drops to half (-3 dB) of the maximum.

## Antenna Gain vs. SWR

Antenna gain is independent of SWR. A poorly matched antenna can still have gain, but some power is lost in reflections before reaching the antenna. Both gain and SWR matter for overall system efficiency.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Antenna gain: measures **concentration of radiated power** vs. reference
- dBi: gain vs. **isotropic radiator** (theoretical sphere)
- dBd: gain vs. **half-wave dipole**
- dBi = dBd + **2.15**
- ERP: transmitter power × **antenna gain**
- Higher gain = **narrower beamwidth**
- Gain does not create power — it **redistributes** it
- Antenna must be **pointed** toward target for high-gain antennas
