# B-001-014: International Operations and Visiting Operators

## Canadian Operators Abroad

When a Canadian amateur radio operator is operating outside Canada, they are subject to **the regulations of the country they are in** — not Canadian regulations. Operating in Florida means following US FCC rules. Operating offshore (e.g., in international waters near the US coast) follows the rules of the nearest jurisdiction's call area.

**CEPT Licence**: Canada participates in the CEPT (European Conference of Postal and Telecommunications Administrations) amateur radio licensing framework. A Canadian operator with **Advanced Qualification** can obtain a CEPT licence and operate in participating countries. When operating in a CEPT country using voice, identification is: **visited country prefix + "stroke" + Canadian call sign** (e.g., "DL/VA3XYZ").

**Operating in the United States**: A Canadian amateur requires only **Basic Qualification** to operate in the US. When using voice in the US, identification is: **Canadian call sign + "portable" or "mobile" + the prefix for the US call area being visited** (e.g., "VA3XYZ portable W4").

## Foreign Operators in Canada

Foreign operators may operate in Canada if their country has an agreement with Canada **and** they have obtained the appropriate permit. CEPT licence holders are granted **Advanced** operating privileges in Canada.

US operators operating in Canada must indicate their **location by city and province** at least once during a contact. They also identify with their US call sign + "mobile" or "portable" + the Canadian call sign prefix for their geographic location.

## Third-Party Communications — International

Canada does **not** prohibit international third-party communications. Canadian stations may handle such messages with any amateur station worldwide, unless the **foreign administration has filed an objection with the ITU**.

Third parties in an international contact are both non-certified persons on each end of the link. Both are considered third parties.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Operating in another country: follow **that country's** regulations
- Operating offshore near the US: follow **US rules**
- CEPT licence: requires **Advanced Qualification**; ID as **country prefix/stroke/call sign**
- Operating in the US: requires only **Basic**; ID as **call sign + portable/mobile + US area prefix**
- Foreign operators in Canada: need agreement + permit; CEPT holders get **Advanced** privileges
- US operators in Canada: must state **city and province** at least once per contact
- International third-party traffic: permitted unless the **foreign administration has objected to the ITU**
