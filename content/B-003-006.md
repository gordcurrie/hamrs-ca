# B-003-006: SSB Transmitter Stages

## The SSB Generation Problem

Single sideband is a form of AM with two modifications: the carrier is suppressed (saving power), and one of the two sidebands is filtered out (saving bandwidth). The challenge is generating this cleanly at the final transmit frequency. The standard approach is to generate SSB at a low, fixed frequency where sharp filtering is feasible, then frequency-shift it to the operating frequency.

## Stage-by-Stage

**Microphone**: Picks up voice audio.

**Speech amplifier**: Amplifies microphone audio. **Microphones usually have a low power output** — the speech amplifier brings the level up to what the balanced modulator needs. It also processes the audio to keep it within the 300–3000 Hz voice range.

**Fixed RF oscillator**: Produces an RF carrier at a fixed, low frequency. This doesn't change when you tune the radio — it's fixed to match the IF filter characteristics.

**Balanced modulator**: Mixes the audio and the RF carrier. Its key property: it **suppresses the carrier and passes the two sidebands** — unlike a conventional AM modulator which passes the carrier plus both sidebands. The output is a double sideband suppressed carrier (DSB-SC) signal. This is the stage that **transposes the voice from the audio spectrum to the radio spectrum**.

**Sideband filter**: Removes the **unwanted sideband**. Because the balanced modulator outputs both sidebands symmetrically around the (suppressed) carrier, the filter selects one and rejects the other. Tuned **near the fixed RF oscillator frequency** — this is why the oscillator is fixed; the filter has to be matched to it.

**Variable frequency oscillator (VFO)**: Generates the signal used to shift the SSB signal from the fixed IF to the desired transmit frequency. **Allows you to adjust the final transmit frequency** by changing the VFO.

**Mixer**: **Transposes the single-sideband signal to the operating frequency** by mixing it with the VFO output.

**Final amplifier**: Boosts the signal to transmit power. Must be **linear** because SSB carries information in amplitude variations — a non-linear amplifier distorts these and produces unintelligible audio. The final typically includes **SWR protection circuitry** to reduce drive or shut down if the antenna mismatch is too severe.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Speech amplifier needed because: **microphones have low power output**
- Balanced modulator: **suppresses the carrier and passes the two sidebands**
- Balanced modulator: transposes voice from **audio spectrum to radio spectrum**
- Sideband filter: **removes the unwanted sideband**; tuned **near the fixed RF oscillator frequency**
- VFO: **allows adjustment of the final transmit frequency**
- Mixer: **transposes the SSB signal to the operating frequency**
- Final amplifier: must be **linear** (voice unintelligible through non-linear amplifier); includes **SWR protection**
