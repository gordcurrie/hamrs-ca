# B-001-023: Antenna Systems and Public Consultation

## Who Has Authority

The **Minister of Innovation, Science and Industry** has authority over antenna installations, including masts and towers. Municipal zoning cannot override federal spectrum management — but ISED has developed a framework that requires consultation with local land-use authorities.

## The Public Consultation Process

Before installing or modifying an antenna system, you generally must contact the **local land-use authority** (municipality, city, township) to determine their consultation requirements. ISED's policy (CPC-2-0-03) sets out this process.

If the local land-use authority has **no approval process**, you must follow the **default public consultation process** outlined by ISED. This includes notifying neighbours and providing a 30-day public comment period.

## What You Must Address

ISED's default process requires you to address **reasonable and relevant concerns** raised in writing within the 30-day comment period. You don't have to agree with every complaint, but you must engage with legitimate concerns.

## When Public Consultation May Not Be Required

Consultation may not be required if an **exclusion criterion** defined by ISED applies — for example, antenna systems below certain height thresholds or in certain situations may be exempt from the full public consultation process.

A municipality or land-use authority can establish its own process, including its own exclusions. If their process has an exclusion that applies, you may not need to consult at all.

## Dispute Resolution

If you and a stakeholder (not the general public) cannot reach agreement, the **final decision is made by ISED** — the regulator, not the municipality or local authority.

## What You Cannot Do

You **cannot** erect any antenna structure without consulting neighbours or the local land-use authority just because you want to. The size of the antenna structure determines the exclusion threshold set by ISED or the municipality.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Authority over antenna installations: **Minister of Innovation, Science and Industry**
- No local approval process exists: follow **ISED's default public consultation process**
- 30-day public comment period: must address **reasonable and relevant written concerns**
- May skip consultation if: **exclusion criterion by ISED or municipality applies**
- Unresolved disputes (non-public stakeholders): decided by **ISED**
- Process is determined by: the **municipality or local land-use authority** (where they have one)
