# B-002-003: Calling Procedures and Simplex vs. Repeater

## CQ — Voice

A CQ is a general call to any station. Correct format:
- Say **"CQ"** three times
- Say **"this is"**
- Say your **call sign** three times

Example: "CQ CQ CQ, this is VA3RAC VA3RAC VA3RAC, over."

## Answering a CQ — Voice

When responding to a CQ:
- Say the **other station's call sign once**
- Say **"this is"**
- Say **your call sign**

You only repeat the other station's call once when answering — the multiple repetitions are for the initial CQ call, not the response.

## Calling a Specific Station — Voice

When you want to reach a particular station (not a general CQ):
- **Their call sign** + **"this is"** + **your call sign**

This format is the preferred standard.

## Simplex vs. Repeater

**Simplex** means transmitting and receiving on the **same frequency**. It is direct station-to-station communication with no repeater involved.

Use simplex when:
- Signals are reliable between stations without a repeater
- You want to reduce load on the repeater
- You want to verify your simplex capability

To check if you can use simplex instead of the repeater: listen for the other station on the **repeater's input frequency**. If you can hear them directly on that frequency, you can probably talk simplex.

## Avoiding Repeater Input Frequencies

When choosing a VHF/UHF simplex frequency, be careful not to inadvertently use a frequency that is the **input** to a local repeater. If you transmit simplex on a repeater's input, you will key up the repeater and cause interference to users who are monitoring its output.

## Sideband Convention

- **HF voice contacts above 10 MHz** (including 20 metres at 14 MHz): **upper sideband (USB)**
- **HF voice contacts below 10 MHz** (including 80 metres at 3.75 MHz): **lower sideband (LSB)**
- 40-metre voice at 7 MHz: **LSB** (technically below 10 MHz despite the "40 metre" name being confusing)

## Exam-Critical Facts

- CQ: say "CQ" **three times**, "this is", call sign **three times**
- Answering a CQ: other call sign **once**, "this is", your call sign
- Simplex: **same frequency** for transmit and receive
- Check for simplex capability: listen on the **repeater's input frequency**
- Don't pick a simplex frequency that is a **repeater input**
- 20 metres and above: **upper sideband**; 80/40 metres: **lower sideband**
