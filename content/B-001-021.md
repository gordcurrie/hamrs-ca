# B-001-021: ITU Regions and Operating Abroad

## The Three ITU Regions

The ITU divides the world into three regions for the purpose of frequency allocation. Different regions may have different allocations for the same amateur bands.

- **Region 1**: Europe and Africa
- **Region 2**: North and South America (Canada is in Region 2)
- **Region 3**: Asia and the Pacific (Australia, Japan, Southeast Asia)

When you travel and operate abroad, the band limits that apply are those of the region and country you are in.

## Operating in Your Home Country vs. Abroad

**In Canada**: Canadian band limits apply.

**Offshore (e.g., in a boat near the US coast)**: The rules of the nearest jurisdiction's call area apply. 7 km offshore from Florida = US rules apply.

## CEPT Operation

The CEPT amateur radio licence framework allows qualified Canadian operators to operate in participating European and other countries with minimal paperwork. Requirements:

- Canadian operator must hold **Advanced Qualification**
- When operating in a CEPT country using voice: transmit **visited country's prefix + "stroke" + Canadian call sign** (e.g., "F/VA3XYZ")
- CEPT countries grant **Advanced** operating privileges to qualifying Canadian visitors

## Operating in the United States

- Canadian Basic Qualification is sufficient — only **Basic** required
- Voice identification format: **Canadian call sign + "portable" or "mobile" + prefix for the US call area**

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Canada is in **ITU Region 2**
- Region 1: **Europe and Africa**; Region 3: **Asia and Pacific** (Australia, Japan)
- Operating offshore near the US: **US rules** apply
- CEPT licence: requires **Advanced**; ID as **country prefix/VA3XYZ**
- Operating in the US: **Basic** sufficient; ID as **VA3XYZ portable/mobile + US area prefix**
