# B-005-012: Resonance

## What is Resonance?

A circuit containing both an inductor and a capacitor has a **resonant frequency** — a frequency at which the inductive reactance exactly equals the capacitive reactance:

> **X_L = X_C** at resonance

At this frequency, the two reactances cancel each other out, and the circuit behaves as if only resistance is present.

## Resonant Frequency Formula

> **f_r = 1 / (2π × √(L × C))**

Where:
- f_r = resonant frequency in Hz
- L = inductance in henries
- C = capacitance in farads

To increase resonant frequency: **decrease L or decrease C**  
To decrease resonant frequency: **increase L or increase C**

## Series Resonance

In a **series LC circuit** at resonance:
- Impedance is **minimum** (only the small series resistance remains)
- Current is **maximum**
- The circuit **passes** signals at the resonant frequency (**bandpass filter** behaviour)

## Parallel Resonance

In a **parallel LC circuit** (tank circuit) at resonance:
- Impedance is **maximum** (theoretically infinite in a lossless circuit)
- Current from the source is **minimum**
- The circuit **rejects** signals at the resonant frequency (**bandstop / band-reject** behaviour)
- Energy circulates between L and C internally

## Q Factor

The **Q factor** (quality factor) describes the sharpness of the resonance:

> **Q = X_L / R** (or X_C / R)

High Q = narrow, sharp resonance peak = selective filter  
Low Q = broad, flat resonance = wider passband

## Applications in Amateur Radio

- **VFO/oscillator**: LC tank determines oscillation frequency
- **Bandpass filters**: series resonant circuits in the signal path
- **Trap antennas**: parallel resonant circuits block current at specific frequencies
- **Tuned circuits** in receivers select desired signals

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Resonance occurs when: **X_L = X_C**
- Resonant frequency formula: **f = 1 / (2π√LC)**
- Series resonance: impedance is **minimum** (maximum current)
- Parallel resonance: impedance is **maximum** (minimum source current)
- To raise resonant frequency: **decrease L or decrease C**
- Q factor: ratio of **reactance to resistance** — higher Q = sharper response
- Parallel LC = **tank circuit** used in oscillators and RF stages
