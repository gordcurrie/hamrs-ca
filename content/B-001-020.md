# B-001-020: International Communications and ITU Framework

## ITU and Canadian Obligations

Canada is a member state of the **International Telecommunication Union (ITU)**, a UN specialized agency. The ITU's Radio Regulations are an international treaty that Canada is bound by. Canadian amateur radio operators must comply with both the Radiocommunication Act/Regulations and the **ITU Radio Regulations**.

This dual obligation is not bureaucratic duplication — the ITU framework establishes the international spectrum allocation structure that makes cross-border communications possible. Without it, every country would do its own thing and international radio would be chaos.

## What International Communications Are Permitted

International communications in the amateur service must be limited to:
- **Purposes of amateur radio** (technical exchange, emergency communication, etc.)
- **Remarks of a personal nature**

Commercial messages, business content, and third-party traffic unrelated to amateur radio purposes are not appropriate in international contacts.

## The Objection Framework

International communications are permitted unless a foreign administration has **registered an objection** with the ITU. If a country has objected through the ITU, Canadian stations may not communicate with amateur stations in that country.

This is the mechanism — the ITU is the body that maintains the registry of objections. You don't need to check a separate Canadian government list; the ITU administers this.

## Morse Code

The ITU Radio Regulations leave Morse code proficiency requirements up to each individual administration to decide. Canada has its own Morse code qualification (5 wpm). The ITU does not mandate a specific speed — each country determines whether Morse is a requirement for authorization.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- International communications: limited to **amateur radio purposes** or **personal remarks**
- Prohibited with countries that have filed an **objection with the ITU**
- ITU provisions applicable to Canadians: **international communications are permitted unless an administration has registered an objection**
- Morse code proficiency: **administrations determine their own requirements** (ITU does not mandate a speed)
- Second organization whose regulations Canadian amateurs must follow: **International Telecommunication Union**
