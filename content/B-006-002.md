# B-006-002: Coaxial Cable

## What is Coaxial Cable?

**Coaxial cable** (coax) consists of a central conductor surrounded by a dielectric insulator, then a braided or foil outer conductor (shield), and finally an outer jacket. The outer conductor completely surrounds the inner — this **shielding** confines the RF field inside the cable, preventing radiation or pickup from external signals.

## Why Coaxial Cable?

- **Shielded**: RF stays inside; the cable does not radiate and is immune to external interference
- **Flexible**: easy to route through buildings and around obstructions
- **50 Ω characteristic impedance**: matches most amateur radio equipment directly

## Common Coax Types

| Type    | Impedance | Diameter | Use                        |
|---------|-----------|----------|----------------------------|
| RG-8    | 50 Ω      | Large    | HF runs, lower loss        |
| RG-8X   | 50 Ω      | Medium   | HF/VHF compromise          |
| RG-58   | 50 Ω      | Small    | Short HF/VHF runs          |
| RG-213  | 50 Ω      | Large    | HF runs (low loss)         |
| RG-59   | 75 Ω      | Medium   | TV/cable systems           |
| LMR-400 | 50 Ω      | Large    | VHF/UHF (very low loss)    |

## Loss in Coaxial Cable

Loss is stated in **dB per 100 feet (or per 100 metres)** at a given frequency. Coax loss:
- Increases with **frequency** (higher frequencies have more loss per unit length)
- Increases with **length** (more cable = more loss)
- Decreases with **larger diameter** cable

For VHF/UHF work, use the lowest-loss cable you can afford.

## Connector Types

- **PL-259 (UHF connector)**: common on HF and VHF amateur equipment; technically misnamed "UHF"
- **N connector**: superior performance at VHF/UHF and microwave; weatherproof
- **BNC connector**: quick-connect for lab use, short VHF runs
- **SMA**: very small; used in handheld radios

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Coaxial cable: **inner conductor, dielectric, outer shield, jacket**
- Shield function: **prevents radiation and pickup** (keeps RF confined)
- Amateur radio standard impedance: **50 Ω**
- Cable loss: increases with **higher frequency** and **greater length**
- Lower loss = **larger diameter** cable
- PL-259 connector: common on **HF/VHF** amateur equipment
- N connector: preferred for **VHF/UHF and above**
