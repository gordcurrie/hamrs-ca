# B-004-005: Vacuum Tubes

## What a Vacuum Tube Does

A **vacuum tube** (valve) is an older amplifying device that controls current flow through a vacuum using electric fields. Though largely replaced by transistors, tubes are still used in high-power amplifiers and prized for audio applications.

## The Diode Tube

The simplest tube has two elements:
- **Cathode**: heated element that emits electrons by **thermionic emission** (heating metal releases electrons)
- **Anode (plate)**: positive electrode that attracts emitted electrons

Current flows from cathode to plate when the plate is positive. The tube diode rectifies AC just like a semiconductor diode.

## The Triode

Adding a third element — the **control grid** — between cathode and plate creates a **triode**. The grid is a mesh of wire that the electrons must pass through. A small **negative voltage on the grid** repels electrons and reduces plate current. A less negative (or slightly positive) grid voltage increases plate current.

Triode terminals:
- **Cathode** — electron emitter
- **Control grid** — voltage-controlled input
- **Plate (anode)** — current output

This is exactly analogous to the BJT: grid ↔ base, cathode ↔ emitter, plate ↔ collector.

## Beyond the Triode

- **Tetrode**: adds a **screen grid** between control grid and plate; reduces capacitive feedback and increases gain
- **Pentode**: adds a **suppressor grid** to eliminate secondary emission from the plate; widely used in transmitters

## Vacuum Tubes Today

Still used in:
- High-power HF/VHF linear amplifiers (kilowatt-class)
- Certain microwave devices (magnetrons, klystrons, TWTs)
- High-fidelity audio amplifiers

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Cathode function: emits electrons by **thermionic emission** (heated)
- Plate (anode): attracts electrons; **positive relative to cathode**
- Triode elements: **cathode, control grid, plate**
- Grid control: **negative grid voltage reduces plate current**
- Grid analogy to BJT: grid ↔ **base**, cathode ↔ emitter, plate ↔ collector
- Tetrode addition: **screen grid**
- Pentode addition: **suppressor grid**
