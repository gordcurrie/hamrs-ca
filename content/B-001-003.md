# B-001-003: Offences and Penalties

## The Legal Framework

Offences and penalties relating to radiocommunications are set out in the **Radiocommunication Act** itself — not the Regulations, not ISED policy documents. Parliament put the penalty teeth in the statute.

## Key Prohibited Acts

**Transmitting outside amateur radio bands** is prohibited. If you do it, penalties may be assessed against the control operator — not just a warning.

**False or fraudulent messages** — transmitting a false distress signal (e.g., falsely saying "MAYDAY" when there is no emergency) is a serious offence. The legal term for this is a "false or fraudulent message." The penalty is a fine not exceeding **$5,000**, or imprisonment not exceeding **one year**, or both.

**Causing harmful interference** — intentionally obstructing or interfering with radio communications without lawful cause carries the same penalties: up to **$5,000 fine** or **one year** imprisonment, or both.

**False information** is never permitted under any circumstances — there is no exception.

## Certificate Suspension

The Minister may suspend an Amateur Radio Operator Certificate, but there are procedural constraints — the operator must be given **notice and an opportunity to make representations**. Suspension without notice or opportunity to respond is **not permitted**.

## Inspector Powers

Radio inspectors have significant authority, but they cannot enter a **dwelling** without the consent of the occupant **and** without a warrant. They can enter business premises and station locations more freely, but a private home retains Charter protections.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Penalties for interference or false signals: fine up to **$5,000** or prison up to **one year**, or both
- Transmitting false information is **never** permitted
- Penalties are in the **Radiocommunication Act**
- Certificate suspension requires **notice and opportunity to make representations**
- Inspectors **cannot** enter a dwelling without consent **and** a warrant
