# B-002-008: Emergency Operating Procedures

## Distress, Urgent, and Safety — Priority Order

Radio communications have a priority hierarchy:
1. **Distress** — immediate danger to life (highest priority)
2. **Urgent** — safety concern, not immediate threat to life
3. **Safety** — navigational or weather safety information
4. **Normal traffic** — everything else

A distress message comes before all others, including emergency messages. If you are in distress, your communication takes priority over everything on the frequency.

## Distress Signals

- **Voice**: say **"MAYDAY"** three times — this is the internationally recognized distress signal
- **Morse code**: send **SOS** (· · · — — — · · ·) — the standard CW distress signal

These signals may only be used in **life-threatening distress situations**. Using them falsely is a criminal offence (see B-001-003: up to $5,000 fine or one year imprisonment).

## Not Immediately Life-Threatening: Urgent

If you are stranded but not in immediate danger (e.g., vehicle breakdown in a safe location), your message is **urgent**, not distress. Different signal, lower priority.

## Using a Repeater in an Emergency

To break into a repeater conversation for an emergency:
- **Break in between transmissions** stating your call sign and situation
- Don't wait for a polite opening — announce your emergency promptly

## Emergency Power and Equipment

The value of operating without commercial AC power: you can **provide communications during a power outage** — exactly when emergency communications are most needed.

For hand-held radios, the most critical additional equipment in an emergency: **an adequate supply of charged batteries**.

Best antenna choice for a portable emergency HF station: **a dipole or a vertical** — simple, effective, deployable anywhere.

## If You Hear Distress and Can't Help Directly

- **Contact authorities** (RCMP, Coast Guard, etc.)
- **Maintain watch** in case your assistance is needed later

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Distress: **MAYDAY** (voice) or **SOS** (CW) — life-threatening situations only
- Priority order: **distress > urgent > safety > normal**
- "Urgent" = safety concern, **no immediate risk to life**
- Breaking into repeater for emergency: **break in between transmissions, state call sign and situation**
- Emergency station hears distress: **acknowledge, determine location and what assistance is needed**
- Value of battery backup: **communications during commercial power outage**
- Best portable emergency HF antenna: **dipole or vertical**
