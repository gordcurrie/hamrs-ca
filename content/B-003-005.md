# B-003-005: SSB/CW Receiver Stages

## Architecture Overview

An SSB/CW receiver is a superheterodyne receiver with some additions specific to recovering suppressed-carrier signals. The stages follow the same fundamental superhet chain, with key additions at the detection stage.

## Stage-by-Stage

**Antenna**: Converts electromagnetic waves into electrical currents. The starting point — it picks up the arriving radio wave and turns it into a tiny electrical signal.

**RF amplifier**: Increases the **sensitivity** of the receiver. Weak signals from distant stations are amplified enough to be usable before mixing.

**Mixer**: Converts the received signal to the **intermediate frequency**. Combines the incoming RF signal with the local oscillator output; the difference (or sum) is the IF.

**Local oscillator**: Generates the signal mixed with the incoming RF to produce the IF. Its frequency determines which RF frequency is received.

**IF filter**: Provides **most of the selectivity** of the receiver — the ability to separate the desired signal from adjacent interference. For SSB, typically 2.4 kHz wide; for CW, 250–500 Hz.

**IF amplifier**: Provides **most of the receiver gain**. Works at the fixed IF frequency.

**Product detector**: Recovers the transmitted modulation for SSB and CW signals. Unlike AM detectors, the product detector **needs the BFO** signal to reconstruct the missing carrier. It multiplies the IF signal by the BFO signal to produce audible audio.

**Beat frequency oscillator (BFO)**: Generates a signal mixed with the IF in the product detector to **recover the transmitted modulation**. For SSB, tuned close to the suppressed carrier frequency. For CW, offset by a comfortable audio beat note (typically 600–800 Hz).

**AF amplifier**: Increases the level of the recovered audio to drive a speaker or headphones. May include an **audio band-pass filter** to further improve selectivity — particularly useful for CW reception, where a 750 Hz band-pass filter cuts adjacent interference dramatically.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Antenna: converts **electromagnetic waves into electrical currents**
- RF amplifier: increases **receiver sensitivity**
- Mixer: converts received signal to the **intermediate frequency**
- Local oscillator: mixed with incoming signal to **produce the IF**
- IF filter: provides **most of the receiver's selectivity**
- IF amplifier: provides **most of the receiver's gain**
- Product detector: **recovers the transmitted modulation**
- BFO: its signal is mixed with the IF to **recover the transmitted modulation**
- AF amplifier: **increases level of recovered modulation** — may include audio band-pass filter
