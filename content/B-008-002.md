# B-008-002: Harmonics and Spurious Emissions

## What are Harmonics?

When a signal at frequency **f** passes through a non-linear device, energy is produced at integer multiples: **2f** (second harmonic), **3f** (third harmonic), **4f** (fourth harmonic), etc. These harmonics can fall on other amateur bands or other services.

**Example**: Operating on 14 MHz (20 m):
- 2nd harmonic: 28 MHz (10 m amateur band — legal but must be weak)
- 3rd harmonic: 42 MHz (TV band)
- 4th harmonic: 56 MHz (TV band)

## Why Harmonics Occur

Harmonics are generated by **non-linearity** in amplifiers. Class C and Class B amplifiers are inherently non-linear and produce harmonics. Even Class A amplifiers produce some harmonics. A **transmitter final amplifier** without adequate low-pass filtering will radiate harmonics.

## Harmonic Suppression Requirements

ISED regulations require that spurious emissions (including harmonics) be suppressed to a specified level below the fundamental. The general requirement for amateur transmitters is:
- At least **40 dB** below the fundamental power (for transmitters above a few watts)
- This means a 100 W transmitter may radiate no more than **10 mW** in harmonics

## Low-Pass Filter

The primary tool for harmonic suppression is a **low-pass filter (LPF)** installed at the **transmitter output** (between the transmitter and the antenna). The LPF passes the fundamental but attenuates all harmonics:
- Cut-off frequency set just above the operating frequency band
- A proper LPF can provide 40–60 dB of harmonic attenuation

## Other Spurious Emissions

Beyond harmonics, transmitters can generate:
- **Parasitic oscillations**: oscillations in the PA at unintended frequencies; suppressed by careful circuit design (parasitic suppressors on tube PAs)
- **Intermodulation products**: mixing products from two or more signals processed simultaneously; related to the linearity of the PA

## Testing for Spurious Emissions

Use a **spectrum analyzer** or **panoramic adapter** to view the transmitter output across a wide frequency range. This reveals harmonics and other spurious signals that cannot be seen with a normal power meter.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Harmonic: energy at **integer multiples** of operating frequency (2f, 3f, 4f…)
- Harmonics caused by: **non-linearity** in amplifier stages
- Suppression requirement: harmonics must be **at least 40 dB below fundamental**
- 100 W transmitter: harmonic power must not exceed **10 mW** (−40 dB)
- Low-pass filter: installed **between transmitter and antenna** to attenuate harmonics
- Parasitic oscillation: unwanted oscillation in **PA stage** at unintended frequency
- Tool to check harmonics: **spectrum analyzer**
