# B-005-001: Metric Prefixes and Scientific Notation

## Why Metric Prefixes?

Electronic quantities span enormous ranges — from picofarads to farads, from milliwatts to kilowatts. Metric prefixes are shorthand for powers of 10 that keep numbers human-readable.

## The Prefix Table

| Prefix | Symbol | Power of 10 | Multiplier         |
|--------|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| pico   | p      | 10⁻¹²      | 0.000 000 000 001 |
| nano   | n      | 10⁻⁹       | 0.000 000 001     |
| micro  | μ      | 10⁻⁶       | 0.000 001         |
| milli  | m      | 10⁻³       | 0.001             |
| (unit) | —      | 10⁰        | 1                 |
| kilo   | k      | 10³        | 1 000             |
| mega   | M      | 10⁶        | 1 000 000         |
| giga   | G      | 10⁹        | 1 000 000 000     |

## Common Radio Examples

- **1.8 MHz** = 1 800 000 Hz = 1.8 × 10⁶ Hz
- **100 pF** = 0.000 000 000 1 F = 100 × 10⁻¹² F
- **2.2 kΩ** = 2 200 Ω
- **500 mW** = 0.5 W
- **3.5 μH** = 0.0000035 H

## Converting Between Prefixes

To convert, move the decimal point:
- 1500 kHz → MHz: divide by 1000 → **1.5 MHz**
- 0.047 μF → nF: multiply by 1000 → **47 nF**
- 2200 Ω → kΩ: divide by 1000 → **2.2 kΩ**

## Exam-Critical Facts

- pico (p) = **10⁻¹²** (one trillionth)
- nano (n) = **10⁻⁹** (one billionth)
- micro (μ) = **10⁻⁶** (one millionth)
- milli (m) = **10⁻³** (one thousandth)
- kilo (k) = **10³** (one thousand)
- mega (M) = **10⁶** (one million)
- giga (G) = **10⁹** (one billion)
- 3.5 MHz = **3 500 000 Hz**
- 1000 mW = **1 W**
- 100 pF = **0.0000000001 F**
