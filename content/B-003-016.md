# B-003-016: Batteries

## Battery Basics

A battery converts chemical energy into electrical energy. It has a **positive terminal and a negative terminal** — the polarity that drives current through an external circuit.

A **storage battery** (secondary battery) can be **repeatedly recharged** by supplying it with electrical energy. Primary batteries (alkaline, etc.) are not rechargeable.

A battery is a source of **electromotive force (EMF)** — the driving force that pushes current through a circuit.

## Vehicle Battery Voltage

A standard automobile starter battery is nominally **12 volts** (actually ~12.6 V fully charged, ~10.5 V discharged).

## Battery Chemistry

**NiMH (Nickel-Metal Hydride)**: preferred over conventional alkaline batteries because it **can be repeatedly recharged**. Used in handheld transceivers and portable equipment.

**Lithium-ion (Li-ion)**: Primary advantages:
- **High battery capacity per kilogram** — the best energy density of common rechargeable types, critical for portable operation
- Never short-circuit a lithium-ion battery — it can cause fire or explosion

**Lead-acid**: Heavy, lower energy density than lithium — not preferred for portable use.

## Battery Voltage Drop Under Load

When a battery supplies current, its terminal voltage drops below the open-circuit voltage due to **internal resistance**. The voltage drop = current × internal resistance. High internal resistance means significant voltage sag under load.

## Series and Parallel Combinations

**Two 12V, 20 Ah batteries in series**: voltage doubles, capacity stays the same → **24 V, 20 Ah**

**Two 12V, 20 Ah batteries in parallel**: voltage stays the same, capacity doubles → **12 V, 40 Ah**

## Battery Capacity and Discharge Rate

Battery capacity is stated as ampere-hours at a specified discharge rate. **Exceeding that current** causes the battery to discharge **more rapidly than specified** — the effective capacity decreases at high discharge rates (Peukert effect).

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Battery: has **positive and negative terminals**
- Storage battery: can be **repeatedly recharged**
- Vehicle starter battery: **12 volts** (nominal)
- NiMH advantage over alkaline: **can be repeatedly recharged**
- Li-ion primary advantage for portable use: **high capacity per kilogram**
- Li-ion: never **short-circuit**
- Voltage drop under load cause: **internal resistance**
- Two 12V/20Ah in parallel: **12V, 40 Ah**
- Two 12V/20Ah in series: **24V, 20 Ah**
- Exceeding rated current: battery discharges **more rapidly than specified**
