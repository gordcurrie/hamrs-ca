# B-002-006: Signal Reports — RST System

## What RST Means

**RST** stands for:
- **R — Readability** (1–5 scale)
- **S — Signal Strength** (1–9 scale)
- **T — Tone** (1–9 scale, CW only)

Voice contacts use RS (two digits). CW contacts use RST (three digits).

## Readability Scale (R)

- **1**: Unreadable
- **2**: Barely readable, occasional words
- **3**: Readable with considerable difficulty
- **4**: Readable with practically no difficulty
- **5**: Perfectly readable

## Signal Strength Scale (S)

- **1**: Faintly perceptible
- **5**: Fairly good strength
- **7**: Moderately strong
- **9**: Extremely strong

## Interpreting Common Reports

- **"5 7"**: Perfectly readable, moderately strong — a solid signal
- **"3 3"**: Readable with considerable difficulty, weak — a struggling signal
- **"5 9 plus 20 dB"**: Perfectly readable, 20 dB stronger than S9 — an extremely strong signal

## The S-Meter and Decibels

The IARU recommends calibrating S-meters at **6 dB per S-unit** above S9, with 50 µV at the antenna input equalling S9. Using this standard:

- Each S-unit = 6 dB = power ratio of 4:1
- Reducing power from 100 W to 25 W = 6 dB reduction = 1 S-unit drop
- Reducing power by 10x = 10 dB reduction

So if someone reads you at S9 at 100 watts, dropping to 25 watts drops you to S8. Going from S9+20 dB to S9 requires reducing power by a factor of 100 (10 dB = factor of 10, 20 dB = factor of 100).

## Reporting Through a Repeater

When a distant station asks for a signal report on a repeater you are monitoring: report **the quality of the station's signal and audio as heard through the repeater** — not what you think their signal might be if you heard them directly.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- RST: **Readability, Signal strength, Tone**
- Voice: use **RS** (two digits); CW: use **RST** (three digits)
- "5 7" = **perfectly readable, moderately strong**
- "3 3" = **readable with considerable difficulty, weak**
- S-meter calibration: **6 dB per S-unit** (IARU standard)
- One S-unit drop = **divide power by 4** (6 dB)
- Report through repeater: what you hear **through the repeater**
- RST 579: perfectly readable, moderately strong, **perfect tone**
