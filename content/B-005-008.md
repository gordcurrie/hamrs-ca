# B-005-008: Inductors

## What is an Inductor?

An **inductor** (coil, choke) is a component that stores energy in a **magnetic field**. It is formed by winding wire into a coil — either air-core or around a magnetic core (iron, ferrite).

The property of an inductor is **inductance (L)**, measured in **henries (H)**. Practical RF inductors are often in the microhenry (μH) or nanohenry (nH) range.

## How an Inductor Behaves

An inductor **opposes changes in current**:
- When current is increasing, the inductor generates a back-EMF opposing the increase
- When current is decreasing, the inductor generates a voltage to sustain the current
- With steady DC: an inductor looks like a short circuit (just wire resistance)
- With AC: the inductor presents **inductive reactance** (opposes AC flow)

This property — opposing change — is called **self-inductance**.

## Inductive Reactance

The opposition an inductor presents to AC is:

> **X_L = 2π × f × L**

Where:
- X_L = inductive reactance in ohms (Ω)
- f = frequency in Hz
- L = inductance in henries

As frequency increases, inductive reactance **increases**. As L increases, reactance increases. An inductor blocks high frequencies more than low — opposite to a capacitor.

## Combining Inductors

- **Series inductors**: L_total = L1 + L2 + … (values add, assuming no mutual inductance)
- **Parallel inductors**: 1/L_total = 1/L1 + 1/L2 + …

## Inductor Applications

- **RF chokes**: block RF from entering DC power supplies
- **Resonant circuits**: combined with capacitors to form filters and oscillators
- **Matching networks**: impedance transformation
- **Energy storage**: in switching power supplies

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Inductor stores energy in: **magnetic field**
- Inductance unit: **henries (H)**
- Inductive reactance: **X_L = 2πfL**
- As frequency increases, X_L: **increases**
- Inductor opposes: **changes in current**
- At DC: inductor is essentially a **short circuit** (low impedance)
- At high frequency: inductor is **high impedance**
- Series inductors: values **add**
