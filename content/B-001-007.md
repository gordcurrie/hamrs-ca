# B-001-007: Permitted and Prohibited Communications

## What Amateur Radio Is For

The amateur service has a defined purpose: technical self-training, intercommunication, and technical investigation. It is not a general-purpose communications service. Several specific types of communication are explicitly prohibited.

## Business Communications

**Business communications are not permitted under any circumstance.** This is firm. You cannot conduct business on the air — no buying or selling, no commercial arrangements, nothing of a business nature. This applies even occasionally. A club net cannot discuss business planning.

## Broadcasting

**Broadcasting to the general public is not permitted.** Amateur radio is a two-way communication service. One-way transmissions intended for anyone who might be listening (broadcasting) are prohibited, with one narrow exception: safety of life and property communications.

Permitted one-way communications include:
- Beacon transmissions
- Emergency/safety communications
- Radio control of model craft (unidentified transmissions are allowed for model control)

## Secret Codes and Encoding

You may use abbreviations (like Q codes) and procedural signals — but **only if they are not secret**. The key word is "secret." Published codes that anyone can look up are fine. Private codes or encryption that obscures the meaning of your message from a third party are prohibited.

A new digital encoding technique you developed may be used only when it is **published in the public domain** — once it's public, it's no longer secret.

Encoded messages may only be sent **when the encoding or cipher is not secret**.

## Retransmitting Non-Amateur Signals

Do not let non-amateur audio (music, a radio broadcast) enter your transmitted signal. Turn down the background volume. Retransmitting broadcast signals is not permitted.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- **Business communications**: never permitted, under any circumstance
- **Broadcasting to the general public**: not permitted (safety communications excepted)
- **Secret codes**: prohibited — codes must be public/published
- **Q codes**: permitted (they are published and not secret)
- **Unidentified transmissions**: only permitted for controlling model craft
- New digital modes may be used only when **published in the public domain**
- Background music/audio must not be transmitted — turn it down
