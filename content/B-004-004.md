# B-004-004: Field-Effect Transistors (FETs)

## What a FET Does

A **field-effect transistor (FET)** is a three-terminal transistor that is **voltage-controlled** rather than current-controlled. A voltage applied to the control terminal modulates current flow through the device. FETs draw almost no input current, making them ideal for high-input-impedance amplifier stages.

## Terminals

- **Gate (G)** — the control terminal (analogous to base in BJT)
- **Drain (D)** — current flows in here (analogous to collector)
- **Source (S)** — current flows out here (analogous to emitter)

## Types of FETs

**JFET (Junction FET)**:
- Gate is a reverse-biased PN junction
- **Depletion mode** only: gate voltage reduces (depletes) the channel current
- N-channel JFET: gate must be negative relative to source; drain current decreases as gate goes more negative

**MOSFET (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor FET)**:
- Gate is insulated from the channel by a thin oxide layer — draws essentially zero gate current
- Can operate in **depletion mode** (reduce channel) or **enhancement mode** (increase channel)
- Enhancement-mode MOSFETs are common in power circuits and digital logic

## Why Use a FET Over a BJT?

- **Very high input impedance** — gate draws near-zero current; does not load the source circuit
- **Low noise** — excellent for weak-signal RF preamplifiers
- **Square-law response** — more naturally linear for certain mixer applications

## FET Amplifiers

FETs are widely used as:
- **Low-noise preamplifiers** (LNAs) for receiver front ends
- **Mixers** in receivers
- **RF power amplifiers** (power MOSFETs — lateral DMOS, LDMOS)

## Exam-Critical Facts

- FET terminals: **gate, drain, source**
- FET is **voltage-controlled** (vs. BJT which is current-controlled)
- JFET gate draws: **essentially zero current** (reverse-biased junction)
- MOSFET gate draws: **essentially zero current** (oxide insulation)
- JFET operation: gate voltage **controls drain current**
- Advantage of FET: **very high input impedance**
- N-channel JFET: gate voltage becomes more negative → drain current **decreases**
