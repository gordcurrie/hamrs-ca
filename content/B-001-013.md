# B-001-013: Station Identification

## The Fundamental Rule

Every amateur radio station must identify itself by transmitting its **call sign**. The call sign is your station's legal identity on the air.

## When to Identify

- At the **beginning** of each contact
- At the **end** of each contact
- At intervals **not greater than 30 minutes** during a contact

If you are in a long conversation or net, you must identify at least every 30 minutes. The 30-minute clock resets with each identification.

## One Exception

**Model craft control**: You may transmit unidentified (without a call sign) only for the purpose of controlling a model craft (drone, RC aircraft, RC boat, etc.). This is the sole exception.

## Call Sign Format — Canadian Prefixes

Valid Canadian amateur call signs begin with: **VA, VE, VO, or VY**

A valid example: **VA3RAC** — prefix VA, numeral 3 (Ontario), suffix RAC.

## Language

Call signs may be transmitted in **English or French**. Both are official languages and either is permitted for identification purposes.

## Test Transmissions

The identification rules for test transmissions are **the same as for regular contacts**. There is no relaxed rule for testing. If you are transmitting, you must identify.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Identify at the **beginning** and **end** of each contact, and every **30 minutes** during a contact
- The call sign is the required identification
- Maximum interval without identification: **30 minutes**
- Unidentified transmissions permitted **only** for controlling a model craft
- Language for identification: **English or French**
- Canadian prefixes: **VA, VE, VO, VY**
- Test transmission identification rules: **same** as regular contacts
