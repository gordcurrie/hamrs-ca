# B-001-002: Amateur Radio Operator Certificates

## What the Certificate Is

The Amateur Radio Operator Certificate is the official authorization to operate an amateur radio station in Canada. It is issued by ISED and has no expiry date — it is valid for **life**.

## Address Changes

You are required to notify ISED within **30 days** of any change to your mailing address. The certificate must be retained at the address on file with ISED. This matters for regulatory enforcement — an inspector needs to be able to contact you.

## Keeping and Showing the Certificate

- The certificate must be **retained at the station** (at the address on file with ISED)
- If a radio inspector asks to see it, you have **48 hours** to produce it (or a copy)
- You do not need to carry it on your person at all times, but you must be able to produce it within 48 hours

## Fees

This surprises many people:
- **First certificate**: Free
- **Replacement call sign** (e.g., due to moving to a new province): Free
- **Changing an existing call sign** (e.g., upgrading to a two-letter call sign): **$60**

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Certificate is valid for **life** (no renewal required)
- Address changes must be reported within **30 days**
- Inspector must receive certificate within **48 hours** of request
- Certificate must be retained at the address provided to ISED
- First certificate: **free**
- New call sign prefix due to address change: **free**
- Choosing a new/different call sign: **$60**
