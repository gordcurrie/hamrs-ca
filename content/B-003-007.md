# B-003-007: Digital Modes and Sound Card Interfaces

## Computer-Based Digital Modes

Modern digital modes (FT8, RTTY, PSK31, WSPR, etc.) are typically generated and decoded by software running on a PC. The audio-based modes work by generating audio tones that are fed into the transceiver's microphone or line input, and decoding received audio from the transceiver's speaker or line output.

## The Sound Card Interface

The sound card interface is the hardware bridge between a computer and a transceiver for digital mode operation. Its functions:

**Audio coupling (both directions)**:
- **Receive**: Converts the analog audio signal from the transceiver into a digital signal for the computer (A/D conversion via the sound card)
- **Transmit**: Converts the digital signal from the computer into an analog audio signal that can be transmitted

**PTT switching**: Switches the transceiver between receive and transmit mode when the software wants to send. Most interfaces include a VOX-triggered PTT or a serial port/USB PTT line.

**Isolation transformers**: Most interfaces use isolation transformers between the transceiver and computer audio connections. Purpose: **prevent hum and interference** from the coupling between devices. Without isolation, ground loops between the PC power supply, the transceiver power supply, and the audio connections create audible 60 Hz hum in the transmitted signal.

## Transceivers with Built-In Sound Cards

Some modern transceivers incorporate an **audio codec** (essentially a sound card) internally. This eliminates the need for a separate sound card interface — the transceiver can connect directly to a computer via USB for digital mode operation.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Sound card interface (receive): converts **received analog audio from transceiver into digital signal** for computer
- Sound card interface (transmit): converts **digital signal from computer into audio** for transmission
- Sound card interface: also **switches transceiver between receive and transmit**
- Sound card interface: provides **audio frequency coupling** between computer and transceiver
- Isolation transformers: prevent **hum and interference** from coupling between transceiver and computer
- Transceivers with no separate sound card: incorporate an **audio codec** internally
