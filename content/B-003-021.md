# B-003-021: RF Safety — Exposure and Biological Effects

## How RF Energy Affects Tissue

RF energy absorbed by body tissue **heats the tissue**. At the frequencies and power levels used in amateur radio, this is the primary mechanism of concern — not ionizing radiation, not electrical shock, but dielectric heating (like a microwave oven, just less intense).

The body organ most likely to be damaged from RF heating: **the eyes**. The eyes have limited blood circulation relative to other tissues and cannot dissipate heat as effectively. The lens of the eye is particularly susceptible to RF-induced cataracts. This is why safety guidelines for UHF and microwave work specifically focus on keeping antennas away from the eyes.

## Power Density and Distance

In free space, the power density of an electromagnetic wave decreases as **the square of the distance** from the antenna. Double the distance → power density drops to one quarter. This is the inverse square law.

This makes distance your most effective tool for reducing RF exposure: moving away from a high-power antenna rapidly reduces exposure to safe levels.

## UHF and Microwave Precautions

- **Keep antenna away from your eyes when RF is applied** — UHF and microwave fields are most hazardous to the eyes
- Before removing shielding from a UHF power amplifier: **make sure the amplifier cannot accidentally be turned on**
- A UHF transmitting antenna must be placed **where no one can get near it when transmitting**

## Hand-Held Transceivers

For hand-held transceivers:
- Hold the antenna **away from your head and away from others** during transmission
- The antenna of a hand-held should not be close to your head when transmitting — to **reduce exposure to RF energy**

## Indoor Antennas

If operating with indoor antennas, locate them **as far away as possible from living spaces** that will be occupied while operating. The combination of proximity and lack of outdoor separation can produce elevated field strengths in adjacent rooms.

## Directional Antennas

High-gain directional antennas should be **mounted higher than nearby structures** so they will not direct RF energy toward people in nearby buildings. A Yagi aimed at a neighbouring house at close range could produce significant field strengths inside it.

## Dipole Placement

For best RF safety, the ends and centre of a dipole should be **as high as possible** to prevent people from coming in contact with the antenna or its near field. The ends of a dipole have high voltage; the centre feedpoint has high current — both areas have elevated field strengths.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- RF energy effect on tissue: **heats the tissue**
- Most likely organ to be damaged: **eyes**
- Power density vs. distance: decreases as the **square of the distance**
- UHF safety: keep antenna **away from eyes** when RF is applied
- Before removing UHF amp shielding: ensure amp **cannot accidentally be turned on**
- Hand-held antenna position: **away from head and away from others**
- Indoor antennas: locate **as far as possible from occupied living spaces**
- Directional antennas: mount **higher than nearby structures**
- Dipole placement: ends and centre **as high as possible**
