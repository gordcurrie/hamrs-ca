# B-004-002: Diodes

## What a Diode Does

A **diode** is a two-terminal semiconductor device that allows current to flow easily in one direction but blocks it in the other. It is the simplest semiconductor device.

The two terminals are:
- **Anode** (A) — the positive terminal; current enters here in forward bias
- **Cathode** (K) — the negative terminal; current exits here in forward bias

A diode **conducts** when the anode is more positive than the cathode (**forward biased**). It **blocks** when the cathode is more positive than the anode (**reverse biased**).

## Rectification

The primary use of a diode is **rectification** — converting AC to pulsating DC by blocking the negative half-cycles. This is the first step in a DC power supply.

- **Half-wave rectifier**: one diode; passes only positive half-cycles
- **Full-wave rectifier**: four diodes in a bridge; converts both half-cycles to positive pulses

## Detection (Demodulation)

A **detector** uses a diode to demodulate AM signals. The diode rectifies the RF envelope, and a capacitor smooths it to recover the audio. The diode acts as a rectifier at RF frequencies.

## Zener Diodes

A **Zener diode** is designed to operate in **reverse breakdown** at a precise, stable voltage — the **Zener voltage**. Once the reverse voltage reaches the Zener voltage, the diode conducts and holds that voltage nearly constant.

**Use**: Voltage regulation. A Zener in parallel with a load keeps the load voltage stable regardless of variations in input voltage or load current. It is a simple voltage reference.

## LEDs (Light-Emitting Diodes)

A **light-emitting diode (LED)** emits light when forward-biased. The colour of light depends on the semiconductor material. LEDs are used as indicators and increasingly as lighting. They require a series resistor to limit current.

## Reverse Polarity Protection

A diode connected in series with a DC supply protects against **reverse polarity** — if the supply is connected backwards, the diode blocks current and prevents damage to the circuit.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Diode terminal names: **anode** (positive) and **cathode** (negative)
- Diode conducts when: **anode is more positive than cathode** (forward biased)
- Diode primary use: **rectification** (AC to DC)
- Zener diode use: **voltage regulation** (operates in reverse breakdown)
- LED: emits light when **forward biased**
- Detector circuit: uses a diode for **AM demodulation**
- Reverse polarity protection: series diode **blocks reverse current**
