# B-001-001: The Radiocommunication Act and Regulatory Framework

## The Hierarchy

Canadian amateur radio is governed by a three-tier legal structure:

- **The Radiocommunication Act** — the primary statute passed by Parliament. It grants the Minister of Innovation, Science and Economic Development (ISED) the authority to regulate all radiocommunication in Canada. This is the top of the chain.
- **The Radiocommunication Regulations** — subordinate regulations made under the Act. These define the rules in detail: licensing, qualifications, operating conditions. This document defines what an "amateur radio service" actually is in Canadian law.
- **Standards for the Operation of Radio Stations in the Amateur Radio Service** — ISED's technical and operating standards. Published under authority granted by the Act.

**ITU Radio Regulations** also apply to Canadian operators — Canada is a member of the International Telecommunication Union and is bound by the treaty framework, particularly for international communications and frequency allocations.

## Why It Matters

The Act is not just a technicality. It establishes:
- ISED as the responsible department (not the CRTC, not Transport Canada)
- The legal basis for issuing certificates and call signs
- The penalty framework for violations
- Inspector powers

When exam questions ask "which document assigns authority to…" the answer is always **the Radiocommunication Act**. When they ask what defines the amateur radio service, it's the **Radiocommunication Regulations**.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- The **Radiocommunication Act** grants authority to make Canadian regulations governing radiocommunications
- The **Radiocommunication Act** also grants authority to publish the Standards for Operation
- **Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED)** is the department responsible for administering the Act
- The **Radiocommunication Regulations** define the Canadian "amateur radio service"
