# B-003-018: Electrical Safety — Shock and Hazards

## Preventing Unauthorized Use

**Home station**: A **key-operated on/off switch in the main power line** is the most effective way to prevent unauthorized use. It prevents the equipment from being powered at all without the key.

**Mobile station**: Remove the **microphone** when you are not using it. A vehicle radio without a microphone cannot transmit — simpler and cheaper than installing a lock.

## Vehicle Battery Hazard

The electrical hazard presented by a starter battery: **high short-circuit current**. A 12V car battery is harmless at 12V (below the hazard threshold), but can deliver hundreds or even thousands of amperes into a short circuit. This level of current can instantly arc-weld metal, start fires, or cause explosion of battery gases. The voltage is not the hazard — the available current is.

## High-Voltage Power Supply Safety

A cabinet interlock switch that cuts power when the cabinet is opened protects against **dangerous high voltages** inside the power supply (tubes, filter capacitors charged to high voltage). The capacitors may remain charged even after the supply is unplugged — this is why you must discharge filter capacitors before working inside.

## Lethal Current Levels

The **minimum electrical current that can be fatal**: **20 milliamperes (20 mA)**. This is the threshold at which ventricular fibrillation can occur. Most household circuits can deliver amperes — thousands of times the lethal threshold. It's the current through the body that kills, not the voltage per se.

**Body organ most vulnerable**: **The heart**. Even small currents passing through the chest can disrupt cardiac rhythm and cause fibrillation.

**Lowest voltage usually considered hazardous**: **30 volts**. Below 30V, the skin resistance is typically sufficient to limit current to safe levels. Above 30V, wet skin or contact with moist mucous membranes can allow dangerous currents.

## Responding to Electrical Injury

**Person being burned by high voltage**: Turn off the power, call for emergency help, and provide first aid if needed. **Do not touch the person while they are in contact with the voltage source** — you could become a second victim.

**Unconscious person in contact with high voltage**: **De-energize the power source before touching the person**. Do not pull them away while the source is live — if the victim is part of the circuit, so are you.

**Before checking faults in a mains power supply**: Turn off power and **unplug the power cord** first.
