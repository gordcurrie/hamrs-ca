# B-003-009: Yagi Antenna Elements

## The Three-Element Yagi

A Yagi (Yagi-Uda) antenna consists of a driven element connected to the feedline, plus parasitic elements that modify the radiation pattern. The three-element version — reflector, driven element, director — is the basic form.

## Elements

**Boom**: The horizontal structural tube or pipe that supports all the elements. It is for **mechanical support** only — the boom carries no RF current (when properly constructed with non-conductive or isolated element-to-boom connections). It is not a radiating element.

**Reflector**: The **longest** radiating element. Longer than the driven element, so it is slightly inductive at the operating frequency. Positioned behind the driven element, it reflects energy forward, concentrating radiation in the forward direction.

**Director**: The **shortest** radiating element. Shorter than the driven element, slightly capacitive. Positioned in front of the driven element, it pulls energy forward, further concentrating the beam.

**Driven element**: Connected to the **transmission line** (feedline). This is the only element that receives power directly from the transmitter. All others are parasitic — induced into activity by the driven element's field.

## How Parasitic Elements Work

The reflector and director are not connected to the feedline. They are energized by induction (near-field coupling) from the driven element's electromagnetic field. The slight difference in length compared to the driven element causes a phase shift in the current they carry, which creates the directional pattern through constructive and destructive interference.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Boom: **mechanical support** only — not a radiating element
- Reflector: **longest** element — positioned behind the driven element
- Director: **shortest** element — positioned in front of the driven element
- Driven element: **connected to the transmission line**
- Parasitic elements (reflector and director): energized by **induction or radiation** from the driven element — not directly connected to feedline
