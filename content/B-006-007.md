# B-006-007: Vertical Antennas and Ground Planes

## The Quarter-Wave Vertical

A **vertical antenna** is a conductor oriented vertically. The most basic is the **quarter-wave vertical (λ/4)**, which is exactly half of a dipole. It uses the **ground** (or an artificial ground plane) as the "missing" other half.

Length of a quarter-wave vertical:

> **Length (metres) ≈ 71.5 / f(MHz)**

**Example**: 7 MHz → 71.5 / 7 ≈ **10.2 metres**

## Ground Plane and Radials

The quarter-wave vertical requires a good RF ground beneath it to function properly. Without it, the antenna presents a poor feedpoint impedance and radiates inefficiently.

**Radials** are horizontal wires extending outward from the base of the antenna at or below ground level. A system of four or more radials provides an acceptable ground plane. Many radials (32+) approach a "perfect" ground.

A **ground-plane antenna** for VHF/UHF has quarter-wave radials drooping downward from the feedpoint at about 45° — this angles the ground plane to present a 50 Ω feedpoint impedance directly.

## Feedpoint Impedance

- Quarter-wave vertical over a **perfect ground**: ≈ **36 Ω**
- Quarter-wave vertical over a **ground-plane with angled radials**: ≈ **50 Ω**
- Five-eighths wave vertical: higher impedance, common at VHF/UHF

## Radiation Pattern

A vertical antenna radiates in a **donut (omnidirectional)** pattern — equal in all horizontal directions. The radiation angle is low (near the horizon), making verticals good for **DX** (long-distance) contacts via low-angle radiation.

## Advantages of Vertical Antennas

- **Omnidirectional** — no need to rotate for different directions
- **Vertical polarization** — matches VHF/UHF FM repeater convention
- **Compact** — one quarter-wave instead of half-wave across
- **Suitable for mobile** — easy to mount on a vehicle

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Quarter-wave vertical length: **71.5 / f(MHz)** metres
- Ground plane provides: the **missing half** of the antenna (electrical ground)
- Radials: improve ground plane; **four minimum** for acceptable operation
- Pattern: **omnidirectional** in the horizontal plane
- Low radiation angle: good for **DX** (long-distance) communication
- Ground-plane antenna with angled radials: feedpoint ≈ **50 Ω**
- Polarization: **vertical**
