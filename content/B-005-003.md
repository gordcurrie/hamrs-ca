# B-005-003: Ohm's Law

## The Foundation

**Ohm's Law** states that the voltage across a conductor is proportional to the current through it, with the constant of proportionality being the resistance:

**V = I × R**

Where:
- **V** = voltage in volts (V)
- **I** = current in amperes (A)
- **R** = resistance in ohms (Ω)

Rearranging gives the other two forms:
- **I = V / R** (current = voltage divided by resistance)
- **R = V / I** (resistance = voltage divided by current)

## The PIE Triangle

A useful memory aid — write V (or E for EMF) on top, I and R on the bottom. Cover the one you want:
- Cover V: I × R
- Cover I: V / R
- Cover R: V / I

## Worked Examples

**Example 1**: A 12 V battery connected to a 100 Ω resistor. Current = 12 / 100 = **0.12 A (120 mA)**

**Example 2**: 200 mA through a 470 Ω resistor. Voltage = 0.2 × 470 = **94 V**

**Example 3**: 50 V drives 25 mA. Resistance = 50 / 0.025 = **2000 Ω (2 kΩ)**

## Units

- Voltage: **volts (V)**
- Current: **amperes (A)** — note: mA must be converted to A before calculating
- Resistance: **ohms (Ω)**

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Ohm's Law: **V = I × R**
- Current formula: **I = V / R**
- Resistance formula: **R = V / I**
- Doubling resistance with fixed voltage: current is **halved**
- Doubling voltage with fixed resistance: current is **doubled**
- 12 V across 50 Ω: I = 12/50 = **240 mA**
- Always convert mA to A (divide by 1000) before substituting into formulas
