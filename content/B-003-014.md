# B-003-014: Transceiver Accessories and Controls

## Electronic Keyer

An **electronic keyer** helps form good Morse code characters by **regulating the lengths of the dits and dahs** — maintaining the correct 1:3 dit-to-dah ratio automatically. The operator controls the speed; the keyer ensures the element timing is precise. This is why most serious CW operators use a keyer with a paddle rather than a straight key.

## Microphone Gain

Before using a microphone for the first time with a transceiver, adjust the **microphone gain level** — set it so the ALC reads correctly on speech peaks without over-driving the transmitter.

## VOX — Voice Operated Transmit

**VOX (Voice Operated Transmit / Voice Operated Switch)** is a circuit that **automatically switches the transmitter to transmit mode when the operator speaks into the microphone**. When the operator stops speaking, VOX releases and the transceiver returns to receive. This allows hands-free operation — useful when logging, writing, or operating mobile.

## Speech Processor

A **speech processor** compresses the dynamic range of the audio before it enters the transmitter. Effect: the average power increases, making the signal more intelligible at the receiving end in marginal conditions. If a transmitter is already at 100% modulation, a speech processor **increases the average power** without increasing peak power.

## DSP Noise Reduction

**DSP (Digital Signal Processing) noise reduction** analyzes noise and signal characteristics to **partially remove noise** from the received audio. It cannot fully eliminate noise, but can substantially improve readability in poor conditions.

## Noise Blanker

A **noise blanker** circuit recognizes **high-amplitude, short-duration pulses** and removes them. Most effective against **short-duration impulse-type noise** — ignition noise from vehicles, electric fences, power line arcing. The noise blanker detects the pulse before it reaches the audio stages and blanks (mutes) the receiver momentarily during the pulse.

## Transmit/Receive Switching

The T/R (transmit/receive) switch in a transceiver **enables one antenna to be used for both transmitting and receiving**. Internally, it routes the antenna to the transmitter PA during transmit and to the receiver input during receive. Without it, you'd need separate antennas or manual switching.

## Dynamic Microphone

A **dynamic microphone** has internal components similar to a loudspeaker — a coil in a magnetic field. Sound moves the coil, which generates a voltage. It is the reverse of a speaker (which takes voltage and moves a coil to produce sound). Robust, doesn't require phantom power.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Electronic keyer: **regulates lengths of dits and dahs**
- First setup with microphone: adjust **microphone gain level**
- VOX: **automatically transmits when operator speaks** into microphone
- Speech processor: **improves intelligibility**; at 100% modulation increases **average power**
- DSP noise reduction: **analyzes noise and signal to partially remove noise**
- Noise blanker: removes **short-duration impulse-type noise**
- T/R switching: **enables one antenna** for both transmit and receive
- Dynamic microphone: internal components **similar to a loudspeaker**
