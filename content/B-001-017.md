# B-001-017: Transmitter Power Limits

## The Fundamental Power Rule

Amateur radio operators must always use **the minimum legal power necessary to communicate**. This is not just good practice — it is a regulatory requirement. Transmitting at maximum power when a fraction of that would work is a violation of the spirit and letter of the regulations.

## Where Power Is Measured

Power limits apply at the **output of the transmitter or external amplifier** — not at the antenna, not in the feedline. If you have a power amplifier, the limit applies to its output.

## Power Limits by Qualification

**SSB (single sideband) — peak envelope power (PEP):**

| Qualification | Max PEP |
|--------------|---------|
| Basic only (no Honours, no Advanced, no Morse) | 560 watts |
| Basic + Honours (80%), or Basic + Morse | 560 watts |
| Advanced Qualification | **2250 watts** |

**Non-SSB emissions (carrier power):**

| Qualification | Max Carrier Power |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Basic (without Advanced) | 190 watts |
| Advanced | limited by DC input rules |

**DC input power to the final RF stage:**

| Qualification | Max DC Input |
|--------------|-------------|
| Basic | 250 watts |
| Advanced | 1000 watts |

## Special Cases

**60-metre band (5 MHz)**: Advanced holders are limited to **100 watts ERP (effective radiated power)** regardless of other power limits.

**630-metre and 2200-metre bands**: Power limits are expressed as **EIRP (equivalent isotropically radiated power)**. To comply, you must account for the antenna **gain** — a highly efficient antenna radiates more EIRP at the same transmitter power. These bands have very low EIRP limits.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Always use **minimum power necessary** to communicate
- Power is measured at the **transmitter or amplifier output**
- Advanced: up to **2250 watts PEP** (SSB)
- Basic without Advanced: **560 watts PEP** (SSB), **190 watts** carrier (non-SSB)
- DC input: Basic = **250 watts**, Advanced = **1000 watts**
- 60 metres: Advanced max = **100 watts ERP**
- 630m/2200m: limits are **EIRP** — antenna gain must be accounted for
