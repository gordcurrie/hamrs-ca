# B-001-006: Station Authorization and Permitted Communications

## Who Can Operate Your Station

An amateur radio station may be used to communicate with **stations operated under similar authorizations** — meaning other amateur radio stations. You cannot use your amateur station to communicate with, say, a marine radio station or a land mobile station on their licensed frequencies.

Even a low-power station (2 watts or less) must be **under the supervision of a person holding an Amateur Radio Operator Certificate and call sign**. Power level does not change the authorization requirement.

## Using Amateur Equipment Outside Amateur Bands

This is an absolute prohibition. Amateur radio equipment may **never** be used to transmit outside amateur radio bands, with or without modification. Amateur equipment is not type-certified for other services — it is not approved for aeronautical, marine, or land mobile frequencies under any circumstances.

Similarly, you may **never** use an amplifier to amplify the output of a licence-exempt transmitter. Licence-exempt devices (like Wi-Fi routers or unlicensed FRS radios) are approved for specific low power levels. Amplifying them takes them outside their authorization, regardless of where you transmit.

## Dual-Use Radios

Some VHF/UHF FM radios sold for the amateur service can also be programmed to operate on land mobile frequencies. This is **only permissible** if the radio is separately **certified and licensed for use in the land mobile service**. Buying an amateur radio and programming it for land mobile frequencies without that certification is not legal.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Amateur stations may only communicate with **stations operating under similar authorizations**
- A 2-watt station must still be **supervised by a certificate holder**
- Amateur equipment may **never** be used outside amateur bands — no exceptions
- Amplifying a licence-exempt transmitter's output is **never permitted**
- Dual-use radios on land mobile frequencies require separate **certification and licensing** for that service
