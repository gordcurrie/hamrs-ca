# B-003-001: HF Station Components

## The Signal Chain

A complete HF station connects several components between the transceiver and the antenna. Understanding what each one does and where it sits in the chain matters both for operation and for the exam.

Typical signal path (transmit, no external amplifier):

**Transceiver → Low-pass filter → SWR meter → Antenna switch → Antenna tuner → Antenna**

With an external power amplifier:

**Transceiver → External amplifier → Low-pass filter → SWR meter → Antenna switch → Antenna tuner → Antenna**

## Low-Pass Filter

Reduces harmonic emissions — signals at multiples of the operating frequency that the transmitter inevitably generates to some degree. By filtering out everything above the amateur band, harmonics cannot reach the antenna and cause interference to TV or FM broadcast receivers.

**Placement**: As close as possible to the output of the transmitter (or external amplifier). Putting it far from the transmitter with an unfiltered cable run between them partially defeats the purpose — the cable itself can radiate harmonics.

Modern HF transceivers have **built-in low-pass filters** in their output stage for exactly this reason.

## SWR Meter

Measures the match between the transmission line impedance and the antenna system impedance. High SWR means energy is being reflected back from the antenna instead of radiated. The SWR meter is inserted **right after the low-pass filter** in the signal chain, so it measures what's actually going to the antenna (not just what left the transmitter).

## Antenna Tuner

Compensates for impedance mismatches between the transmission line and antenna. When changing frequency significantly, antennas that are not wideband become mismatched — the tuner adjusts to restore a good match. Particularly important with **solid-state final amplifiers**, which are sensitive to high SWR and may reduce power or shut down if the mismatch is too severe.

## Antenna Switch

Selects which antenna (or the dummy load) is connected to the transmit path. Simple mechanical switch; nothing exotic.

## Dummy Load

Absorbs RF energy without radiating it. A resistive load (typically 50 Ω) that presents a matched impedance to the transmitter while dissipating power as heat. Used for testing and adjustment without causing on-air interference.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Low-pass filter purpose: **reduce harmonic emissions**
- Low-pass filter location (no external amp): **as close as possible to transceiver output**
- Low-pass filter location (with external amp): **as close as possible to amplifier output**
- SWR meter: inserted **right after the low-pass filter**
- SWR meter purpose: **measure impedance match** between transmission line and antenna
- Antenna tuner: may be needed when **changing frequency** with a solid-state final
- Dummy load: **dissipates RF energy without radiation** — for testing
- Antenna switch: **selects the desired antenna or dummy load**
