# B-001-016: Authorized Frequencies, Bandwidth, and Emission Modes

## Maximum Authorized Bandwidths

Bandwidth limits prevent adjacent-channel interference. The regulations specify maximum occupied bandwidth by frequency range:

| Band Range | Max Bandwidth |
|-----------|--------------|
| 1.8–7 MHz and most HF (7–25 MHz) | **6 kHz** |
| 28–29.7 MHz (10 metres) | **20 kHz** |
| 50–54 MHz (6 metres) | **30 kHz** |
| 144–148 MHz (2 metres) | **30 kHz** |

## Special Case: 30-Metre Band (10.1–10.15 MHz)

The 30-metre band has the most restrictive rules:
- Maximum bandwidth: **less than 6 kHz** (narrower than other HF bands)
- **Single sideband (SSB) is prohibited** on this band
- This band is shared with other services and was granted to amateurs with restrictions

## Prohibited Combinations

Certain mode/frequency combinations are explicitly prohibited:
- **Fast-scan television (ATV) on 14.23 MHz** — the bandwidth would greatly exceed the 6 kHz limit for 20 metres
- **ATV on 145 MHz** — prohibited
- **SSB on 10.12 MHz** — falls in the 30-metre band where SSB is prohibited

## Staying Inside the Band

When transmitting near a band edge, you must ensure the **entire occupied bandwidth** falls within the amateur band — not just the carrier or centre frequency. If your signal is 6 kHz wide and you're 3 kHz from the band edge, part of your signal is outside the band. This is a violation.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- HF bands (7–25 MHz): max **6 kHz** bandwidth
- 10 metres: max **20 kHz**
- 6 metres and 2 metres: max **30 kHz**
- 30-metre band (10.1–10.15 MHz): max **less than 6 kHz**, **SSB prohibited**
- Near band edges: entire **occupied bandwidth** must be within the band
- ATV on 14.23 MHz or 145 MHz: **prohibited**
