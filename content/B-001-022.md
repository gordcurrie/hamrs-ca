# B-001-022: Examinations — Process and Fees

## Where to Take the Exam

Amateur radio qualification examinations in Canada can be taken:
- **At an ISED office**: No charge for qualification examinations
- **With an accredited examiner**: The examiner may charge a fee to recover costs (not a government fee — the examiner sets it)

## No Exceptions for Language or Disability

The exam rules include a couple of potential gotchas:
- A candidate with **insufficient knowledge of English or French** may **not** be accompanied by an interpreter. You must demonstrate proficiency in English or French to take the exam in Canada
- A disabled candidate may **not** be exempted from portions of the examination — accommodations may be available, but the exam content itself cannot be waived

## What Is Free

- **Qualification examinations at an ISED office**: free (no charge)
- There is no per-qualification fee at ISED offices

What is **not** free (from B-001-002): changing your existing call sign costs $60. Replacement due to moving provinces is free.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Exams at ISED office: **no charge**
- Exams with accredited examiner: **fee to recover costs** (examiner may charge)
- Interpreter for language barrier: **not permitted**
- Exam exemption for disability: **not permitted** (content cannot be waived)
