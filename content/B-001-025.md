# B-001-025: Electromagnetic Compatibility — EMCAB-2

## What EMCAB-2 Is

**Electromagnetic Compatibility Advisory Bulletin 2 (EMCAB-2)** is ISED's guidance document for determining responsibility when amateur radio transmissions cause interference to nearby electronic devices. It establishes a field strength criterion to decide who is "at fault."

## The Core Framework

EMCAB-2 distinguishes between different types of affected equipment and sets an **immunity criterion** — a field strength level below which the equipment is expected to work correctly:

- **Associated equipment**: Devices used in home entertainment systems that are not strictly radio apparatus (TVs, stereos, audio amplifiers, DVD players). There is a defined field strength threshold for these.
- **Radio-sensitive equipment**: Devices that are technically radio apparatus (garage door openers, wireless baby monitors, etc.)

## Determining Responsibility

The key question is: is the field strength of the amateur station's emissions at the neighbour's premises above or below ISED's immunity criterion?

- **Below the criterion**: The neighbour's equipment is insufficiently immune — the **equipment's lack of immunity** is deemed the cause. The amateur is not at fault; the device should meet the standard.
- **Above the criterion**: The amateur's **transmission** is deemed the cause, regardless of the equipment's immunity. The amateur must resolve the interference.

## Practical Implications

This framework prevents neighbours from demanding that a legally operating amateur station cease transmitting simply because their old stereo picks it up. If the amateur's field strength at the property boundary is within legal limits, the onus is on the equipment to be immune.

Conversely, if you are blasting massive RF into a neighbourhood with a kilowatt, you cannot hide behind the criterion — you must resolve the interference.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- EMCAB-2: determines responsibility for **amateur radio interference to nearby devices**
- **Below immunity criterion**: neighbour's device **lacks immunity** — their problem
- **Above immunity criterion**: **amateur's transmission** is the cause — their problem to fix
- **Associated equipment**: entertainment devices (stereo, TV) — not technically radio apparatus
- **Radio-sensitive equipment**: devices that are radio apparatus (garage door openers, etc.)
