# B-001-024: RF Safety — Safety Code 6

## What Safety Code 6 Is

**Safety Code 6** is the Health Canada document that sets the maximum exposure limits for RF energy near the human body. It applies to all radio transmitters in Canada — there are no exemptions. Not hand-held radios, not amateur stations, nothing is exempt from Safety Code 6.

## The Frequency of Greatest Risk

The human body is most vulnerable to RF energy in the frequency range **48 MHz to 300 MHz**. This is because the human body dimensions are resonant with wavelengths in this range — RF energy is absorbed most efficiently at these frequencies. The exposure limit is set lowest in this range precisely because absorption is highest.

Think of it like a resonant antenna: the human body (at roughly 1.7 m tall) has a half-wave resonance around 88 MHz, right in the middle of the most-absorbed range.

## Two Basic Situations

Safety Code 6 specifies limits for two categories of environment:
- **Controlled environments** (occupational exposure — people who know they are exposed and can take precautions)
- **Uncontrolled environments** (the general public — neighbours, bystanders, people who may not know they are being exposed)

Uncontrolled environment limits are lower (more protective) than controlled environment limits.

## Health Effects

Two established adverse health effects from RF exposure are recognized:
1. **Tissue heating** (the primary mechanism at higher frequencies) — absorbed RF energy heats tissue like a microwave oven
2. **Nerve stimulation** (especially below 10 MHz) — at lower frequencies, RF can directly stimulate nerves and muscles

Safety Code 6 addresses both. The limits are set to prevent both effects.

## What Safety Code 6 Does NOT Specify

- Safety Code 6 does **not** specify maximum power output to the antenna of a hand-held radio — power levels vary too much by situation. The limits are field strength-based, not power-based
- It does **not** set limits in terms of power fed into antennas — it sets limits on field strength in the environment

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Safety Code 6: published by **Health Canada**
- Purpose: sets **RF exposure limits** for the human body
- Highest absorption risk: **48 MHz to 300 MHz** (body absorbs most RF in this range)
- Covers two situations: **controlled** and **uncontrolled** environments
- Second health effect (below 10 MHz): **nerve stimulation**
- Hand-held transmitter power limits: **not specified** in Safety Code 6
- No transmitters are **exempt** from Safety Code 6
- Limits are expressed as **field strength**, not antenna power levels
