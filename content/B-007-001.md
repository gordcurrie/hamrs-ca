# B-007-001: Radio Wave Propagation — Overview and Ground Wave

## How Radio Waves Travel

RF energy radiated from an antenna can reach distant receivers by several mechanisms:
1. **Ground wave** (surface wave) — travels along the earth's surface
2. **Sky wave** (ionospheric propagation) — reflects off the ionosphere
3. **Line of sight** (space wave) — travels in a straight line at VHF/UHF
4. **Tropospheric** — refracted by weather in the lower atmosphere

Each mechanism dominates at different frequencies and distances.

## Ground Wave Propagation

The **ground wave** travels along the earth's surface, following its curvature. Key characteristics:

- **Frequencies used**: primarily **MF and lower HF** (below ≈ 3 MHz, most useful below 2 MHz)
- **Range**: up to several hundred kilometres for AM broadcast (530–1700 kHz); shorter at higher frequencies
- **Signal path**: along the surface — no ionospheric involvement
- **Attenuation**: signal decreases with distance and is higher at **higher frequencies**
- **Terrain**: ground wave is attenuated by dry ground (high resistance) and propagates well over **sea water** (high conductivity)

Ground wave is used for:
- AM broadcast stations reaching local coverage
- Maritime mobile communication on MF
- Navigational beacons (NDB)

## Line-of-Sight Propagation (VHF/UHF)

At VHF and above, the primary propagation mode is **line of sight (LOS)**:
- Signal travels in a straight line from transmitting antenna to receiving antenna
- Range is limited by the **radio horizon** — slightly beyond optical horizon due to atmospheric refraction
- **Formula**: approximate range ≈ 3.57 × (√h_t + √h_r) km, where h is antenna height in metres
- Higher antennas → greater range

This is why repeaters are placed on high buildings or mountaintops.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Ground wave frequencies: primarily **MF and lower HF** (below 3 MHz)
- Ground wave propagates: along the **earth's surface**
- Ground wave range: up to **several hundred km** for AM broadcast
- Best ground wave conductivity: **sea water**
- Ground wave attenuation: increases with **higher frequency**
- VHF/UHF primary mode: **line of sight**
- LOS range increases with: **greater antenna height**
- Repeaters on high sites: extend **line-of-sight range**
