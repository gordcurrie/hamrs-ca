# B-001-010: Interference — Rights, Duties, and Protected Bands

## Harmful Interference Defined

**Harmful interference** is interference that seriously degrades, obstructs, or repeatedly interrupts a radiocommunication service. This is a legal term — casual inconvenience doesn't qualify, but repeated obstruction does.

**Deliberate interference is never acceptable.** No circumstances justify intentionally interfering with another station's communications. None.

## Primary vs. Secondary Users

When the Radiocommunication Regulations designate amateur radio as a **secondary user** of a frequency band and another service as a **primary user**, the rule is clear:

- Amateur operators may use the band **only if they do not cause interference to primary users**
- Amateur stations are **not protected** from interference caused by primary users in those bands

This is the fundamental secondary user bargain: you get access, but you yield to the primary service.

## Equal Rights on Amateur Frequencies

When two amateur stations want to use the same frequency, **both have an equal right** to the frequency. Neither has priority (outside of emergency/distress situations). Courtesy and operating practice govern who yields.

## Bands with Heavy Licence-Exempt Activity

The **902–928 MHz** band is heavily occupied by licence-exempt devices (cordless phones, ZigBee, industrial sensors, etc.). Amateur operations in this band:
- Must not cause interference to primary users
- Are **not protected** from interference caused by licence-exempt devices

Similarly, **430–450 MHz** is a band where amateur operations must not cause interference to other radio services operating there.

## Test Transmissions

Test transmissions are permitted but only when they will **not cause interference** to stations in the amateur service or other services. Use a dummy load for bench testing.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- **Harmful interference**: seriously degrades, obstructs, or repeatedly interrupts a service
- **Deliberate interference**: never acceptable, no exceptions
- **Secondary user**: may not interfere with primary users; not protected from them
- **Equal rights**: both stations have equal right to the same frequency
- **902–928 MHz**: heavily occupied by licence-exempt devices; amateurs not protected here
- **430–450 MHz**: amateur operations must not cause interference to other services
- Test transmissions must not cause interference — use a dummy load for testing
