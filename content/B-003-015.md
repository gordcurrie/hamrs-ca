# B-003-015: Digital Modes

## Digital vs. Analog

The **fundamental difference** between digital and analog data: **digital data is encoded as discrete, pre-agreed values** (typically binary: 0s and 1s). Analog data is continuous — it can take any value in a range. Digital allows error detection, compression, and processing that isn't possible with analog.

**Any analog information can be converted to digital data** — voice, images, video, sensor readings. This is why a modern digital radio can transmit voice and images, not just data characters.

## FT8

FT8 (Franke-Taylor design, 8-FSK modulation) is an extremely narrow, weak-signal digital mode. Its bandwidth is much narrower than SSB:
- **Dozens of FT8 communications can occur simultaneously in the space needed for one SSB transmission** — because each FT8 signal is only ~50 Hz wide
- FT8 can work at the **lowest signal-to-noise ratio** of common modes — it functions well below the threshold where SSB becomes unintelligible

## RTTY

RTTY (Radio Teletype) is an older digital mode using FSK. Frequency separation for adjacent RTTY signals: **250–500 Hz centre-to-centre** to minimize interference.

## Packet Radio

Packet radio is a data mode that:
- Has a **network** capability — allows data to be routed over long distances by connecting packet stations
- Implements **ARQ (Automatic Repeat reQuest)** — the protocol requests retransmission of corrupted packets, providing **error correction** (reliable messaging with guaranteed delivery or notification of failure)
- Is especially useful for **emergency communications** because of reliable messaging

A **digipeater** (digital repeater) receives packet data and **retransmits data marked for retransmission** — it extends the reach of the packet network.

## Sound Card Digital Mode Levels

With a computer sound card digital mode:
- **Excessive audio drive** into the transmitter causes **splatter or out-of-channel emissions** — the PA is driven into non-linearity
- Verify correct level: **ask a local station to confirm your signal is free of splatter** or use an SDR receiver to monitor your own signal

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Digital vs. analog: digital data is **discrete pre-agreed values**
- Any analog info can be converted to digital: **yes** — enables voice/image transmission
- FT8: **narrow bandwidth** — dozens fit in one SSB slot
- FT8: lowest SNR mode — works at **lowest signal-to-noise ratio**
- RTTY frequency separation: **250–500 Hz** centre-to-centre
- Packet ARQ: provides **error correction** (reliable messaging)
- Packet for emergencies: **reliable messaging** (guaranteed delivery)
- Digipeater: retransmits **data marked for retransmission**
- Excessive audio in digital mode: **splatter or out-of-channel emissions**
