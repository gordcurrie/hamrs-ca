# B-007-004: Solar Activity and HF Propagation

## The Sun Controls HF

The ionosphere is created and maintained by **solar radiation** — ultraviolet and X-ray energy from the sun ionizes the upper atmosphere. Changes in solar activity directly change ionospheric electron density and therefore HF propagation conditions.

## The Solar Cycle

Solar activity follows an approximately **11-year cycle** between solar minimum and solar maximum:

- **Solar maximum**: many sunspots, high solar flux → high ionospheric electron density → **higher MUF** → better HF propagation, especially on higher bands (10 m, 12 m, 15 m)
- **Solar minimum**: few sunspots, low solar flux → low electron density → **lower MUF** → higher bands often dead; only lower HF bands (40 m, 80 m) reliable

## Sunspot Number

**Sunspot number** is the most commonly used indicator of solar activity. More sunspots = more solar activity = better HF propagation on higher frequencies.

The **Solar Flux Index (SFI)** measured at 10.7 cm wavelength is a more precise measure of solar output. Higher SFI = better HF conditions.

## Solar Flares and Ionospheric Disturbances

**Solar flares** are sudden bursts of X-ray and UV energy that can severely disrupt HF propagation:

- **Sudden Ionospheric Disturbance (SID)**: intense X-ray burst increases D-layer ionization dramatically → HF signals on the sunlit side of Earth are **strongly absorbed** — a HF blackout on the side facing the sun. Can last minutes to hours.

- **Geomagnetic storm**: a **coronal mass ejection (CME)** from the sun arrives at Earth 1–3 days after a flare, disturbing the magnetosphere → polar regions are particularly affected; HF paths over or near the poles are disrupted. Aurora borealis is enhanced. Can last hours to days.

## K and A Index

Two indices measure geomagnetic activity:
- **K index** (0–9): quasi-logarithmic, updated every 3 hours. Higher = more disturbed conditions. K ≥ 5 = geomagnetic storm.
- **A index**: daily average of the K values. Lower is better for HF propagation.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- Solar cycle length: approximately **11 years**
- Solar maximum: **higher MUF**, better propagation on **higher bands** (10–15 m)
- Solar minimum: **lower MUF**, only lower bands (40–80 m) reliable
- Sunspot number: indicator of **solar activity level**
- Sudden Ionospheric Disturbance: caused by **solar flare X-rays** → HF blackout on **sunlit side**
- Geomagnetic storm: caused by **CME** arriving 1–3 days after flare
- K index: 0 = calm, **≥5 = geomagnetic storm** — higher K = worse HF
- Best HF conditions: high solar flux, **low K index**
