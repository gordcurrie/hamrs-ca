# B-002-009: Logging, QSL Cards, Grids, and Time

## QSL Cards

A **QSL card** is a written confirmation of a radio contact between two amateur operators. "QSL" literally means "I confirm receipt of your transmission." Traditionally a physical postcard, now often an electronic entry in online logging systems (LoTW, eQSL, etc.).

**A logbook is NOT a regulatory requirement** in Canada — it is good practice and useful for awards programs, but the regulations do not mandate that you keep a log.

## Grid Squares (Maidenhead Locator)

The Maidenhead Locator System divides the Earth into grid squares identified by a 4 or 6-character code (e.g., FN03, EN52rs). In VHF/UHF contesting and DXing, exchanging grid squares tells each operator exactly where the other is located. A grid square is typically ~1° latitude × 2° longitude (for 4-character squares) or smaller for 6-character.

## Long-Path vs. Short-Path

The Earth is a sphere, so between any two points there are two great-circle paths. The **short path** is the shorter route; the **long path** is the opposite direction — exactly **180 degrees** from the short-path heading. Sometimes the long path produces better propagation than the short path. To try long-path, point your antenna 180° from its normal bearing.

## Azimuthal Maps

An **azimuthal map** is a map projection centred on a specific location. It shows true great-circle bearings from that location to any point on Earth — the bearing you read off the map is the compass heading to point your antenna. The most useful function: **to show the bearing to any location**.

## UTC — Coordinated Universal Time

Amateur radio logs, schedules, and contact confirmations use **UTC (Coordinated Universal Time)**. UTC is measured from the **Greenwich meridian** in the UK.

To convert: if your time zone is UTC−6, subtract 6 hours from UTC to get local time. For a 19:00 UTC net in a UTC−6 zone: 19 − 6 = 13:00 local (1 PM).

## Standard Frequency and Time Stations

**CHU** (Canada), **WWV** and **WWVH** (USA) are radio stations that broadcast **accurate frequency and time signals**. Amateur operators tune to them to calibrate frequency counters and clocks.

## Exam-Critical Facts

- QSL card: **written proof of communication** between two operators
- Logbook: **not a regulatory requirement** in Canada
- Grid square: **4 or 6-character location code** (Maidenhead system)
- Long-path heading: **180 degrees** from short-path
- Azimuthal map: shows the **bearing to any location** from the map centre
- UTC reference meridian: **Greenwich, UK**
- Converting UTC−6 at 19:00 UTC = **1 PM local**
- CHU/WWV/WWVH: **accurate frequency and time signals**
